# Behind Putin Visit, Unease in Beijing Over His Potential Next Stop: North Korea

## Russian leader skips tacking on Pyongyang to his China visit, but growing ties between Putin, Kim worry Xi 俄罗斯领导人跳过了平壤对中国的访问，但普京和金正恩之间日益增长的关系令人担忧 习

Chinese leader Xi Jinping has felt growing unease as ties between Putin and North Korean leader Kim Jong Un—two of his most important but also most volatile international partners—have grown more intimate, the diplomats and officials said. A combined visit to China and North Korea by Putin could also have reinforced Western fears of a [trilateral authoritarian axis](https://www.wsj.com/world/asia/russia-pushes-security-talks-with-north-korea-china-ffb76901?mod=article_inline), leaving Beijing diplomatically more isolated, they said.
外交官和官员说，随着普京和朝鲜领导人金正恩之间的关系变得更加亲密，中国领导人习近平感到越来越不安 - 这是他最重要但也最不稳定的两个国际伙伴。他们说，普京对中国和朝鲜的联合访问也可能加剧西方对三边威权轴心的担忧，使北京在外交上更加孤立。

To China’s relief, Putin didn’t head to Pyongyang straight from the northern Chinese city of Harbin, just some 460 miles away from the North Korean capital. 
令中国松了一口气的是，普京并没有直接从距离朝鲜首都约460英里的中国北方城市哈尔滨前往平壤。

On Saturday, Putin’s spokesman Dmitry Peskov told Russia’s state news agency, TASS, that arrangements for a visit by the Kremlin leader to North Korea are progressing well. “Preparations for the visit are under way,” Peskov said. He didn’t announce a date for the planned trip.
周六，普京的发言人德米特里·佩斯科夫（Dmitry Peskov）告诉俄罗斯国家通讯社塔斯社（TASS），克里姆林宫领导人访问朝鲜的安排进展顺利。“访问的准备工作正在进行中，”佩斯科夫说。他没有宣布计划旅行的日期。

It couldn’t be determined whether Beijing exerted any pressure on Moscow ahead of Putin’s meetings in China, his first foreign trip after [starting a new term](https://www.wsj.com/world/russia/putin-inauguration-russian-president-524636fe?mod=article_inline)earlier this month. But the Chinese side has made clear to the Kremlin that Beijing’s preference is to develop bilateral ties, as opposed to more of a three-way alignment that Russia has previously floated. 
目前尚无法确定北京是否在普京会晤前对莫斯科施加了任何压力，这是他本月早些时候开始新任期后的首次出访。但中方已向克里姆林宫明确表示，北京更倾向于发展双边关系，而不是俄罗斯此前提出的三方结盟。

“China is avoiding the optics of a trilateral cooperation among the three countries,” said Yun Sun, director of the China program at the Stimson Center, a Washington think tank. “The goal is to avoid being locked in by two unpredictable partners.”
“中国正在回避三国之间的三边合作，”华盛顿智库史汀生中心（Stimson Center）中国项目主任孙云（Yun Sun）说。“我们的目标是避免被两个不可预测的合作伙伴锁定。

Putin in September [accepted an invitation from Kim](https://www.wsj.com/world/asia/north-korea-kim-jong-un-military-nuclear-f33ede51?mod=article_inline) to visit North Korea. An analyst from the Russian International Affairs Council think tank in Moscow, which has close ties to the Kremlin, wrote in a recent analysis that after the meeting with Xi, the Russian leader “will consider the possibility of visiting a number of other non-Western capitals,” including Pyongyang.
普京于9月接受了金正恩访问朝鲜的邀请。与克里姆林宫关系密切的莫斯科俄罗斯国际事务委员会智库的一位分析师在最近的一份分析中写道，在与习会晤后，俄罗斯领导人“将考虑访问包括平壤在内的其他一些非西方首都的可能性”。

The diplomatic dance among the three countries, which share an interest in dulling the U.S.’s influence in the world, has grown more complicated as Kim has sought to reduce his country’s reliance on China—most dramatically by wooing Putin with public support and weapons for the Russian war effort.
这三个国家之间的外交舞蹈都希望削弱美国在世界上的影响力，随着金正恩试图减少他的国家对中国的依赖——最引人注目的是用公众支持和武器来吸引普京俄罗斯的战争努力。

Since Beijing and Moscow declared a “[no-limits friendship](https://www.wsj.com/articles/china-russia-xi-putin-ukraine-war-11646279098?mod=article_inline)” just weeks before Russia’s invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, China and Russia have [built on a trade relationship](https://www.wsj.com/articles/xi-jinping-putin-china-russia-relations-11671030896?mod=article_inline) that has become a lifeline to Putin in the face of Western sanctions. Washington and its European allies say China has helped Russia [revive its military production](https://www.wsj.com/world/putin-beijing-visit-xi-support-3b0531c3?mod=article_inline) by providing it with drone engines and other dual-use material—products Beijing labels as part of its “normal trade.” 
自 2022 年 2 月俄罗斯入侵乌克兰前几周，北京和莫斯科宣布建立“无限友谊”以来，中国和俄罗斯建立的贸易关系已成为普京面对西方制裁的生命线。华盛顿及其欧洲盟友表示，中国通过向俄罗斯提供无人机发动机和其他军民两用材料，帮助俄罗斯恢复了军事生产，这些产品被北京标记为其“正常贸易”的一部分。

Still, facing pressure from the Biden administration, Xi has refrained from offering weapons to Putin, according to U.S. and Ukrainian officials.
尽管如此，据美国和乌克兰官员称，面对拜登政府的压力，习没有向普京提供武器。

As the Russian war against Ukraine turns into a drawn-out conflict, Putin has instead [turned to North Korea](https://www.wsj.com/articles/russia-is-again-turning-to-north-korea-to-replenish-ammunition-supplies-d79a5318?mod=article_inline) to help restock his depleted armory. Secretary of Defense Lloyd Austin told lawmakers earlier this month that North Korea’s [munitions and missiles](https://www.wsj.com/world/asia/how-the-ukraine-war-is-aiding-north-koreas-illicit-arms-business-62e66fd8?mod=article_inline) helped the Russians “get back up on their feet.”
随着俄罗斯对乌克兰的战争变成一场旷日持久的冲突，普京转而求助于朝鲜，以帮助补充他枯竭的军械库。美国国防部长劳埃德·奥斯汀（Lloyd Austin）本月早些时候告诉国会议员，朝鲜的弹药和导弹帮助俄罗斯人“重新站起来”。

Both countries have denied arms exchanges, including a rebuttal from Kim’s sister issued in state media on Friday.
两国都否认武器交换，包括金正恩姐姐周五在官方媒体上发表的反驳。

Russia and North Korea have kept Beijing in the dark about what was discussed when Putin and Kim met for [a rare summit in the fall](https://www.wsj.com/world/kim-jong-un-and-vladimir-putin-meet-at-russias-vostochny-cosmodrome-91529be9?mod=article_inline), leading Chinese diplomats to ask their Western counterparts about what was agreed between the duo, according to people familiar with the matter.
据知情人士透露，俄罗斯和朝鲜一直对普京和金正恩在秋季举行罕见峰会时讨论的内容一无所知，导致中国外交官向西方同行询问两人之间达成了什么协议。

Beijing worries that Russia might help China’s erratic neighbor build up its nuclear capabilities, the people said.
知情人士说，北京担心俄罗斯可能会帮助中国这个不稳定的邻国建立核能力。

Russian analysts who follow Moscow’s relations with Pyongyang said Kim’s visit to Russia last year was a landmark event that allowed North Korea to overcome its foreign policy isolation and Russia to continue its pivot to the East.
关注莫斯科与平壤关系的俄罗斯分析人士表示，金正恩去年对俄罗斯的访问是一个具有里程碑意义的事件，它使朝鲜克服了外交政策的孤立，俄罗斯继续向东方靠拢。

“China is very worried about this new entente,” said Dennis Wilder, a former U.S. intelligence officer and now a fellow at Georgetown University.
“中国对这个新协约非常担心，”前美国情报官员、现任乔治城大学研究员丹尼斯·怀尔德（Dennis Wilder）说。

Wilder noted that Beijing, while not willing to try to slow the Kim regime’s missile or satellite launches, has appeared to weigh in to persuade Pyongyang to hold back from conducting another nuclear test. “China wants a North Korea that presents a latent threat in Northeast Asia while maintaining the status quo,” he said.
怀尔德指出，北京虽然不愿意试图减缓金正恩政权的导弹或卫星发射速度，但似乎已经说服平壤不要再进行核试验。“中国想要一个在东北亚构成潜在威胁的朝鲜，同时维持现状，”他说。

Putin last visited Pyongyang in 2000 when he went for discussions with Kim’s father, Kim Jong Il, in a trip that was telegraphed by Moscow as demonstrating to the West the reluctance of the Kremlin to join the collective ostracizing of North Korea. On that trip, Putin traveled directly to Pyongyang from Beijing.
普京上一次访问平壤是在2000年，当时他与金正恩的父亲金正日进行了讨论，莫斯科通过电报向西方表明克里姆林宫不愿加入对朝鲜的集体排斥。在那次访问中，普京从北京直接前往平壤。

Analysts who follow Russian-North Korean relations say Kim has to tread carefully because he needs to preserve ammunition stocks for the defense of his own nation should there be an all-out war on the Korean Peninsula. But Kim is eager to have Putin in his corner, as a possible resource for improving his missile and nuclear programs, the analysts said.
关注俄朝关系的分析人士表示，金正恩必须谨慎行事，因为如果朝鲜半岛发生全面战争，他需要保留弹药库存以保卫自己的国家。但分析人士表示，金正恩渴望让普京站在他的角落，作为改善其导弹和核计划的可能资源。

“Broadly speaking they have immediate desires of each other,” Derek Grossman, a senior defense analyst at the global policy think tank and research institute Rand, said of Putin and Kim. “So it is, I think, a quid pro quo, if you want to look at it through the lens of the war in Ukraine and the lens of what North Korea wants to do vis-à-vis South Korea and Japan and the U.S.”
“从广义上讲，他们对彼此有直接的欲望，”全球政策智库和研究机构兰德（Rand）的高级国防分析师德里克·格罗斯曼（Derek Grossman）在谈到普京和金正恩时说。“所以，我认为，如果你想通过乌克兰战争的镜头以及朝鲜想对韩国、日本和美国做些什么的镜头来看待它，这是一种交换条件。”

One reason why Xi didn’t want Putin to add Pyongyang to his itinerary following his trip to China lies with Beijing’s strategic intention to prevent China from being grouped into an axis that could deepen a demarcation in geopolitics—particularly in Northeast Asia, with China, Russia and North Korea on one side and Japan, South Korea and the U.S. on the other.
习不希望普京在访问中国后将平壤纳入他的行程，原因之一是北京的战略意图是防止中国被归为一个可能加深地缘政治分界线的轴心——特别是在东北亚，一边是中国、俄罗斯和朝鲜，另一边是日本、韩国和美国。

It is little secret in either Beijing or Pyongyang that the relationship between Xi and Kim is less trusting than the ties previous Chinese leaders had with Kim’s father, who was more intent on maintaining stability while challenging the West. The younger Kim since coming to power in 2011 has carried out more than triple the number of missile tests than his father and grandfather combined.
无论是在北京还是在平壤，习和金正恩之间的关系不如前几任中国领导人与金正恩父亲的关系那么信任，后者更倾向于在挑战西方的同时保持稳定。自2011年上台以来，年轻的金正恩进行的导弹试验次数是他父亲和祖父总和的三倍多。

Those kinds of activities make China nervous because Beijing is eager to maintain the strategic buffer zone in North Korea in relation to South Korea, which is aligned with the U.S. and Japan. To do this, Beijing needs stability. 
这些活动让中国感到紧张，因为北京急于维持朝鲜与韩国相关的战略缓冲区，韩国与美国和日本结盟。要做到这一点，北京需要稳定。

But Kim looks like he is more willing “to rock the boat” than his father, which worries Beijing, Rand’s Grossman said. 
但金正恩看起来比他的父亲更愿意“摇摆不定”，这让北京感到担忧，兰德公司的格罗斯曼说。

During Putin’s latest visit to China, he and Xi issued a joint statement with a relatively lengthy section accusing the U.S. of escalating tensions on the Korean Peninsula. The statement urged Washington to “abandon intimidation, sanctions and suppression” of North Korea.
在普京最近一次访华期间，他和习发表了一份联合声明，其中有一段相对较长的部分，指责美国加剧了朝鲜半岛的紧张局势。声明敦促华盛顿“放弃对朝鲜的恐吓、制裁和镇压”。

The Chinese and Russian leaders also pledged to intensify their cooperation. Andrew Belousov, the [new Russian defense minister](https://www.wsj.com/world/russia/as-putin-girds-for-protracted-war-a-new-russian-elite-emerges-meet-the-princelings-1b3f49b4?mod=article_inline), was with Putin on this trip, which analysts say signaled military cooperation likely was at the top of the Russian’s leader’s agenda. In recent months, Moscow has spoken of the benefits of stepping up joint military exercises between the two countries.
中俄两国领导人还承诺加强合作。俄罗斯新任国防部长安德鲁·别洛乌索夫（Andrew Belousov）与普京一起参加了这次访问，分析人士称，这表明军事合作可能是俄罗斯领导人议程的首要任务。近几个月来，莫斯科谈到了加强两国联合军事演习的好处。

For Moscow, what Pyongyang can provide beyond the war in Ukraine is limited, and strengthening the bilateral relationship with Beijing for economic support will be much more valuable to Putin, said John Everard, a former U.K. ambassador to North Korea and Belarus. 
前英国驻朝鲜和白俄罗斯大使约翰·埃弗拉德（John Everard）表示，对莫斯科来说，平壤在乌克兰战争之外所能提供的东西是有限的，加强与北京的双边关系以获得经济支持对普京来说将更有价值。

“North Korea is not a priority for Putin,” Everard said. “North Korea is just having a wild fling with Russia.”
“朝鲜不是普京的优先事项，”埃弗拉德说。“朝鲜只是在与俄罗斯进行疯狂的争吵。”

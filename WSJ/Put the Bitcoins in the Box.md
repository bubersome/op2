### BDR

I began [yesterday’s column](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2024-01-03/coal-plants-for-green-investors?srnd=undefined) by saying that I used to write a lot about crypto, but I am trying to do less of it these days. The main reason, I wrote, is that crypto used to be a laboratory for rediscovering central intuitions about finance: Crypto was building a brand-new alternative financial system from scratch, and crypto enthusiasts were discovering solutions to financial problems for themselves. Sometimes those solutions were better than the traditional solutions, and sometimes they were worse, and sometimes they just used new terminology to re-describe solutions that traditional finance discovered years ago, but in any case they were *fun to write about*. Better, or worse, or the same, they provided a lens through which to see traditional finance; seeing why crypto did something, and why it did or didn’t work, could help you understand why traditional finance does things the way it does.
我昨天的专栏开始时说，我过去经常写关于加密货币，但最近尽量少写。我写道，主要原因是，加密货币曾经是重新发现金融中心直觉的实验室：加密货币正在从零开始构建全新的替代金融系统，加密货币爱好者正在为自己发现金融问题的解决方案。有时这些解决方案比传统解决方案更好，有时更糟，有时只是使用新术语重新描述传统金融多年前发现的解决方案，但无论如何，这些都是有趣的写作对象。无论是更好、更糟还是相同，它们提供了一个透视传统金融的视角；看到为什么加密货币做某事，以及它为什么成功或失败，可以帮助你理解传统金融为什么以其方式行事。

This worked because, in 2020 and 2021, crypto’s *financial system* was developing at a much more rapid pace than, you know, its underlying real-world utility. People were using crypto to build lots of clever and exciting new ways to trade crypto, or trade crypto derivatives, or [front-run crypto trades](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-12-13/the-trump-spac-pipe-is-free-money), or build infinitely leveraged crypto shadow banks, or steal money from customers; all of that was interesting if you were interested in financial systems. If you were interested in useful consumer products crypto was maybe less exciting, but that’s not my beat so I couldn’t tell you.1
这种情况发生是因为在2020年和2021年，加密货币的金融系统发展速度远远快于其基础的现实世界效用。人们利用加密货币构建了许多聪明和令人兴奋的新交易方式，或者交易加密货币衍生品，或者在加密货币交易前进行预先交易，或者建立无限杠杆的加密货币影子银行，或者从客户那里窃取资金；如果你对金融系统感兴趣，所有这些都很有趣。如果你对有用的消费产品感兴趣，加密货币可能就没那么令人兴奋了，但这不是我的关注点，所以我无法告诉你。

And then in 2022 and 2023, the crypto financial system was *fascinating* to write about, because many of its big institutions — culminating in FTX Trading Ltd. — collapsed in clouds of smoke and there were tons of even better stories about, like, why leverage is dangerous and how bankruptcy works and so forth. (I got my start as a financial journalist a few years after the 2008 financial crisis, which was a great time, because there were tons of court cases that brought to light both how the 2000s shadow-banking boom developed and how it collapsed; 2022 was crypto’s 2008, and 2023 was its 2013.)
然后在2022年和2023年，加密金融系统成为了一个令人着迷的写作话题，因为许多大型机构 — 最终以FTX Trading Ltd.为代表 — 瓦解成了一团烟雾，还有许多更好的故事，比如杠杆交易为何危险以及破产是如何运作的等等。（我在2008年金融危机几年后开始从事金融新闻工作，那是一个很好的时机，因为有大量法庭案件揭示了2000年代影子银行业务的发展和崩溃；2022年是加密货币的2008年，2023年则是其2013年。）

But after that collapse, the crypto financial system does seem to have gotten more boring, or at least if it’s still doing fun stuff it is quieter about it. (This, too, is reminiscent of the banking industry after 2008: Banking was supposed to be boring, and mostly it was boring, and when it was interesting it didn’t pay to brag about that.)
但在那次崩溃之后，加密金融系统似乎变得更加无聊，或者至少如果它仍在做有趣的事情，那么它变得更加低调。（这也让人想起了2008年之后的银行业：银行业应该是无聊的，大部分时候确实是无聊的，当它变得有趣时，也不值得吹嘘。）

To the point that if you have read a story about crypto so far in 2024, it is overwhelmingly likely to be about the following topic: “People want to be able to put Bitcoins into a box and then sell shares of the box to regular investors in the regular financial system using their regular brokerage accounts.” [*The*](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-12-27/bitcoin-advances-as-optimism-builds-about-etf-approval-by-sec) [big](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-01-03/bitcoin-btc-reverses-course-ahead-of-eagerly-awaited-etf-decision)[innovation](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/12/26/spot-bitcoin-etf-approval-may-be-coming-in-january-2024.html) in [crypto](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2024/01/04/citi-alumni-founded-startup-to-offer-bitcoin-securities-that-dont-need-green-light-from-sec-bloomberg/#:~:text=10 Years of Decentralizing the Future&text=A group of former Citigroup,and Exchange Commission (SEC).) in early 2024, the thing that has driven up prices, is anticipation that the US Securities and Exchange Commission will approve a [spot Bitcoin exchange-traded](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-08-29/grayscale-can-be-a-bitcoin-etf) fund this month. Then ETF providers — [including](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-12-29/blackrock-names-jane-street-jpmorgan-as-bitcoin-etf-authorized-participants)traditional financial firms like BlackRock and Fidelity — will be able to hold Bitcoins in a pot, and institutional and retail investors will be able to buy and sell shares of that pot to get exposure to Bitcoin’s price without actually owning Bitcoin. This, it is thought, will increase the price of Bitcoin.
到2024年迄今为止，如果你读过有关加密货币的故事，很可能主题是：“人们希望能够把比特币放入一个盒子，然后通过他们的常规经纪账户在常规金融系统中向普通投资者出售该盒子的股份。” 2024年初加密货币的重大创新，推动价格上涨的是美国证券交易委员会将批准一个现货比特币交易所交易基金的预期。然后，包括传统金融公司如贝莱德和富达在内的ETF提供商将能够持有比特币，并且机构和零售投资者将能够买卖该基金的股份，以获得比特币价格的暴露，而无需实际拥有比特币。据认为，这将提高比特币的价格。

If your interest in crypto is “number go up,” this is good. If your interest in crypto is “this is the financial system of the future and will increasingly be adopted by big institutions and ordinary people,” this is a mixed bag: On the one hand, there is a lot of optimism about ETF approval driving retail and institutional adoption; on the other hand, “everyone owns Bitcoin through a BlackRock ETF in their Fidelity brokerage account” is not quite proof that crypto is the financial system of the future. If your interest in crypto is “crypto keeps coming up with fun new ways to do finance,” though, this is pretty boring. Crypto’s fun new way to do finance is to put Bitcoin in a box and sell you shares of the box; the goal is to transmute Bitcoin — this decentralized disintermediated trustless novel form of money that was meant to replace the banks and brokerages — into regular stocks.
如果你对加密货币感兴趣是因为“数字上涨”，那很好。如果你对加密货币感兴趣是因为“这是未来的金融体系，将越来越被大型机构和普通人采用”，那就是一把双刃剑：一方面，人们对ETF批准推动零售和机构采用持乐观态度；另一方面，“每个人都通过Fidelity经纪账户持有比特币的BlackRock ETF”并不能完全证明加密货币是未来的金融体系。然而，如果你对加密货币感兴趣是因为“加密货币不断提出有趣的新金融方式”，那就相当无聊。加密货币提出的有趣新金融方式是将比特币放入一个盒子并出售盒子的股份；目标是将比特币——这种去中心化、无中介、无信任的新型货币，旨在取代银行和经纪公司——转变为普通股票。

Anyway while you wait for the ETF, [here’s Bloomberg’s Muyao Shen](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-01-04/ex-citi-execs-plan-bitcoin-btc-securities-that-don-t-need-ok-from-us):
无论如何，在等待ETF的时候，这里是彭博社的沈慕尧：

> As speculation about the upcoming approval or denial of spot-Bitcoin ETFs reaches a fever pitch, a group of former Citigroup Inc. executives is starting to offer securities backed by the oldest cryptocurrency that they say don’t need the blessing of US regulators.
> 随着有关即将批准或否决现货比特币ETF的猜测达到高潮，一群前花旗集团高管开始提供由最古老加密货币支持的证券，他们称这些证券不需要美国监管机构的祝福。
>
> The new offering, called Bitcoin depositary receipts, will be similar to American depositary receipts that represent foreign stocks. The startup, called Receipts Depositary Corporation, or RDC, said it plans to issue the first Bitcoin depositary receipts to qualified global institutional investors in transactions exempt from registration under the Securities Act of 1933.
> 这项新产品被称为比特币存托凭证，类似于代表外国股票的美国存托凭证。初创公司名为存托凭证公司（Receipts Depositary Corporation，简称RDC），表示计划向符合条件的全球机构投资者发行首批比特币存托凭证，交易免于根据1933年证券法注册。
>
> Known as BTC DRs, the offering will give institutions access to Bitcoin securities through US regulated market infrastructure and cleared through the Depository Trust Co., according to a release from the company. …
> 被称为BTC DRs，该产品将使机构通过美国监管的市场基础设施获得比特币证券，并通过存管信托公司结算。根据公司发布的消息。
>
> Broadridge Corporate Issuer Solutions will serve as the transfer agent and Anchorage Digital Bank National Association will handle custody of the underlying Bitcoin. RDC is backed by investors including Franklin Templeton, BTIG and Broadhaven Ventures, according to its press release. …
> Broadridge Corporate Issuer Solutions将担任过户代理，Anchorage Digital Bank National Association将处理基础比特币的托管。根据其新闻稿，RDC得到了包括富兰克林坦普顿、BTIG和Broadhaven Ventures在内的投资者的支持。
>
> Compared with Bitcoin ETFs that will be redeemed for cash, Mehta said that depositary receipts offer direct ownership of Bitcoin for qualified institutions. Buying Bitcoin directly isn’t the most-preferred option for some regulated institutions, he added, since crypto markets face challenges including security risks and regulatory uncertainty. Some of the challenges are similar to those once seen for Americans investing in foreign companies, which were mitigated by American depositary receipts.
> 与将以现金赎回的比特币ETF相比，Mehta表示，存托凭证为合格机构提供比特币的直接所有权。他补充说，直接购买比特币并非一些受监管机构的首选，因为加密市场面临诸多挑战，包括安全风险和监管不确定性。其中一些挑战类似于曾经美国人投资外国公司时面临的挑战，而美国存托凭证已经缓解了这些挑战。

A spot ETF is probably the most broadly convenient way to put Bitcoins in a box and sell shares of the box, but the basic concept is very simple and obvious and can be implemented in a lot of ways. The ETF implementation has the two advantages that (1) anyone can buy and sell shares of the box and (2) the *box* can easily create or redeem shares for Bitcoins, meaning that the share price of the box really should track the price of Bitcoin. Whereas the leading existing box of Bitcoins, [the Grayscale Bitcoin Trust](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-03-07/ftx-wants-its-bitcoins-back-from-grayscale), has the first advantage — it is pretty freely tradeable by anyone2 — but not the second: For securities law reasons, it can’t freely redeem its shares for Bitcoins, so its share price has diverged widely from the price of Bitcoin.3 (Grayscale also intends to convert into an ETF, when the SEC allows it to.)
一个现货交易所交易基金（ETF）可能是将比特币放入一个箱子并出售箱子股份的最广泛便利的方式，但基本概念非常简单明了，可以通过多种方式实现。ETF的实施有两个优点：（1）任何人都可以购买和出售箱子的股份；（2）箱子可以轻松地为比特币创建或赎回股份，这意味着箱子的股价确实应该跟踪比特币的价格。而目前领先的比特币箱子，Grayscale比特币信托基金，具有第一个优点——任何人都可以相对自由地交易——但没有第二个优点：出于证券法的原因，它不能自由地为比特币赎回其股份，因此其股价与比特币价格出现了明显分歧。（Grayscale还打算在SEC允许的情况下转变为ETF。）

Meanwhile this box — BTC DRs — has the second advantage (easy convertibility and price tracking) but not the first: To avoid the need for SEC approval and registration, it will only sell its shares to institutional investors in private transactions. This box is just “if you are an institutional investor, we will give you Bitcoin wrapped up in a security, so you can hold it in your ordinary custody account and transfer it through the Depository Trust Co. and generally have the experience of holding a stock or bond, not a weird crypto thing, except the stock’s price happens to be the price of Bitcoin.”
与此同时，这个盒子——BTC DRs——具有第二个优势（易于兑换和价格跟踪），但不具备第一个优势：为了避免需要SEC批准和注册，它只会将股份出售给机构投资者进行私人交易。这个盒子就是“如果你是机构投资者，我们将为你提供包装成证券的比特币，这样你可以将其保存在你的普通托管账户中，并通过存管信托公司进行转移，通常会有持有股票或债券的体验，而不是奇怪的加密货币，只是股票的价格恰好是比特币的价格。”

And that strikes me as genuinely useful. Some investors want to be crypto investors, but a lot of institutional investors very much do *not* — they do not want to spend time or money on understanding blockchains or keeping track of private keys or complying with [SEC requirements](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-02-16/the-sec-comes-for-crypto-custody) about [crypto custody](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-01-12/don-t-forget-your-bitcoins) — but still want to own Bitcoin. Owning regular shares of stock that happen to be Bitcoins is, for them, very useful; it domesticates Bitcoin into the regular financial system. It’s just that that’s boring. In 2021, as FTX was heading toward a $32 billion valuation, its ambition was to tokenize all the stocks, become the next Goldman Sachs, rebuild all of the traditional financial system on crypto rails. In 2024, the ambition for crypto is to make Bitcoin a stock, to finally get crypto onto traditional rails.
这让我觉得确实很有用。一些投资者想成为加密货币投资者，但很多机构投资者并不想这样——他们不想花时间或金钱去了解区块链，或者跟踪私钥，或者遵守有关加密货币托管的SEC要求——但仍然想拥有比特币。对他们来说，拥有普通股票，恰好是比特币，非常有用；它将比特币纳入常规金融系统。只是这样很无聊。2021年，当FTX朝着320亿美元的估值迈进时，它的雄心是将所有股票进行代币化，成为下一个高盛，将所有传统金融系统重建在加密铁轨上。到2024年，加密货币的雄心是将比特币变成股票，最终将加密货币引入传统铁轨。

This particular innovation is so old that [I wrote about it back in 2018](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2018-09-10/keep-your-bitcoins-in-the-bank), when these former Citigroup executives were just Citigroup executives, and Citi was going to do the depository receipts: 
这项特定的创新如此古老，以至于我在2018年就写过它，当时这些前花旗集团高管只是花旗集团的高管，而花旗即将进行存托凭证的业务：

> Here is Citigroup Inc. looking at investor demand and concluding: Yes, sure, Bitcoin is great, but what Bitcoin investors really want is to hold Bitcoins in the form of *receipts issued by a giant bank and registered at DTCC*. That’s where the real innovation is! That’s what the people want! “Take this blockchain away from me,” they cry, “and give me the old system that I know!”
> 花旗集团正在关注投资者需求，并得出结论：是的，比特币很棒，但比特币投资者真正想要的是持有由一家巨大银行发行并在DTCC注册的比特币凭证。这才是真正的创新！这才是人们想要的！“把这个区块链拿走”，他们呼喊，“给我我熟悉的旧系统！”

In 2024 that is *obviously* what they want.
在2024年，这显然是他们想要的。

### A DAO 一个DAO

A lot of innovations in crypto have two separate but related rationales:
加密货币领域的许多创新都有两个独立但相关的原因：

1. This thing is the future of human society!
   这个东西是人类社会的未来！
2. This thing avoids securities law!
   这个东西规避了证券法！

I remember when blockchains were touted both as a new way to keep track of all of the world’s information and also, somehow, a way around securities law: If you put stock on the blockchain, the theory went, the US Securities and Exchange Commission couldn’t regulate it. (This theory has fallen out of favor, but [not entirely](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-07-14/ripple-is-a-security-and-it-isn-t).) 
我记得当区块链被吹捧为一种新的方式来跟踪全球所有信息，同时也被视为绕过证券法的一种方式：如果你把股票放在区块链上，理论上，美国证券交易委员会就无法监管它。（这种理论已经不再流行，但并非完全消失。）

Or people in crypto have [spent years arguing](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2016-05-17/blockchain-company-wants-to-reinvent-companies) that decentralized autonomous organizations are a new and better way to organize economic activity, never seen before. (This is wrong and [they are general partnerships](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-10-24/big-bonuses-are-back-at-uk-banks), but never mind.) But there are also sometimes arguments that DAOs are a way around securities regulation: If some crypto project (a decentralized exchange, a lending platform, whatever) is run by a DAO rather than by a company, then it is more robust to regulation; regulators can’t fine the DAO or arrest its leaders, because it is decentralized and computerized and thus resistant to government interference.
加密货币领域的人们多年来一直在争论，认为去中心化自治组织是一种前所未有的新型和更好的组织经济活动的方式。（这是错误的，它们实际上是普通合伙企业，但不要紧。）但有时也会有人争辩说DAO是规避证券监管的一种方式：如果某个加密货币项目（去中心化交易所、借贷平台等）由DAO而不是公司运营，那么它对监管更加强大；监管机构无法对DAO处以罚款或逮捕其领导者，因为它是去中心化和计算机化的，从而抵抗政府干预。

My guess is that this is partly correct: Some decentralized crypto projects really do run as more or less autonomous code on distributed blockchains and really *can’t* be shut down by an SEC order.4 But there do seem to be a lot of [$5 wrench vulnerabilities](https://xkcd.com/538/) in crypto, where “decentralized autonomous organization” is a fancy way of saying “Discord page for voting on stuff,” and the SEC can find the Discord’s administrator and say “hey we can make your life bad if you don’t shut down this DAO” and the administrator shuts it down and everything was a lot less decentralized and autonomous and organized than people thought.
我猜这部分是正确的：一些去中心化的加密项目确实在分布式区块链上以更或少自主的代码运行，确实无法被证券交易委员会的命令关闭。但在加密领域似乎存在许多“5美元扳手”漏洞，所谓的“去中心化自治组织”实际上就是“用于投票的Discord页面”的花哨说法，证券交易委员会可以找到Discord的管理员并说“嘿，如果你不关闭这个DAO，我们会让你的生活变得糟糕”，管理员关闭了它，一切都没有人们想象的那么去中心化、自治和有组织。

Last month the SEC [shut down a DAO](https://www.sec.gov/news/press-release/2023-258):
上个月SEC关闭了一个DAO

> The Securities and Exchange Commission [Dec. 22] announced that BarnBridge DAO, a purportedly decentralized autonomous organization, and its two founders, Tyler Ward and Troy Murray, will pay more than $1.7 million to settle charges that they failed to register BarnBridge’s offer and sale of structured crypto asset securities known as SMART Yield bonds. The Commission also charged the respondents with violations stemming from operating BarnBridge’s SMART Yield pools as unregistered investment companies. To settle the SEC’s charges, BarnBridge agreed to disgorge nearly $1.5 million of proceeds from the sales, and Ward and Murray each agreed to pay a $125,000 civil penalties.
> 证券交易委员会[12月22日]宣布，据称是去中心化自治组织的BarnBridge DAO及其两位创始人Tyler Ward和Troy Murray将支付超过170万美元以解决指控，指控称他们未注册BarnBridge发行和销售名为SMART Yield债券的结构化加密资产证券。委员会还指控被告违反了将BarnBridge的SMART Yield池运作为未注册投资公司的规定。为了解决证券交易委员会的指控，BarnBridge同意返还近150万美元的销售收益，而Ward和Murray各同意支付12.5万美元的民事罚款。
>
> “The use of blockchain technology for the unregistered offer and sale of structured finance products to retail investors runs afoul of the securities laws," said Gurbir S. Grewal, Director of the SEC’s Division of Enforcement. "This case serves as an important reminder that those laws apply to all who wish to access our capital markets, regardless of whether they are, or purport to be, incorporated, decentralized or autonomous.”
> 美国证券交易委员会执法部主任古比尔·S·格鲁瓦尔表示：“利用区块链技术向零售投资者非注册地提供和销售结构化金融产品违反了证券法。”“这起案件提醒人们，无论是谁，无论其是否成立、去中心化或自治，都必须遵守这些法律，以便进入我们的资本市场。”

### Portable credit 便携式信用

We talked a while back about [assumable mortgages](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-09-13/yieldstreet-missed-a-boat). A lot of homeowners in the US have mortgages with 3%-ish interest rates that they got a few years ago, but if you try to get a mortgage today, it will have a 7%-ish interest rate. If I own a house and have a 3% mortgage, and I want to sell it to you and you want to buy it, you will go to a bank and get a 7% mortgage, and your bank will give you the money, and you will give me the money, and I will give the money to my bank to pay off my 3% mortgage. This seems very inefficient. You are borrowing expensive money to give to me to pay off a cheap mortgage early.
我们之前谈过可转让抵押贷款。美国有很多房主的抵押贷款利率大约为3%，是几年前获得的，但如果你今天想申请抵押贷款，利率将达到7%左右。如果我拥有一栋房子，有一笔3%的抵押贷款，而我想把它卖给你，而你想买，你将去银行申请一笔7%的抵押贷款，你的银行会给你钱，你会把钱给我，我会把钱给我的银行，用来提前偿还我的3%的抵押贷款。这看起来非常低效。你在借贵的钱给我，用来提前偿还便宜的抵押贷款。

It would be better for me, and for you, if you could just *assume* my mortgage — if you could move into my house and keep making the 3% payments on my mortgage, instead of making the new 7% payments.5 (It would arguably be worse *for our banks:* My bank would rather get its below-market 3% mortgage paid off early at par, and your bank would rather originate a new market-rate mortgage.6)
如果你能承担我的抵押贷款，搬进我的房子并继续支付3%的贷款，而不是支付新的7%的贷款，对我和你都会更好。这对我们的银行来说可能更糟：我的银行宁愿提前以面值偿还其低于市场利率的3%抵押贷款，而你的银行宁愿发放一笔新的市场利率贷款。

In the US, some mortgages are, with some contortions and hoop-jumping, assumable: Their terms provide that, if we recite exactly the right incantations, you can take over my mortgage and keep paying 3%. But most aren’t. And a problem in the US housing market now is that everyone who has a 3% mortgage can’t move, because they’d need to get a 7% mortgage somewhere else, which constrains supply.
在美国，一些抵押贷款是可以转让的：只要我们按照一定的程序，你就可以接管我的抵押贷款并继续支付3%的利率。但大多数抵押贷款是不可转让的。目前美国房地产市场的一个问题是，所有拥有3%抵押贷款的人无法搬家，因为他们需要在其他地方获得7%的抵押贷款，这限制了供应。

Private equity buyouts also work that way? Like the way a private equity buyout works is that a fund borrows a lot of money from lenders to pay some of the purchase price for the company, and then it pays down those loans over time. And if it sells the company to another private equity firm while the loans are still outstanding, then ordinarily the loans come due immediately, so the new private equity buyer has to go to *its* lenders to get new loans to pay the old private equity owner the money to hand over to *its* lenders. And just as with mortgages, if the old owner got its loans during a period of low rates and easy credit, and the new buyer has to get its loans during a period of high rates and more difficult credit, some trades won’t get done: The company is worth more to the old owner, with its cheap debt, than it will be to a new owner with expensive debt.
私募股权收购也是这样运作的吗？就像私募股权收购的运作方式是，一家基金从贷款人那里借了很多钱来支付公司的部分购买价格，然后随着时间的推移偿还这些贷款。如果在贷款仍未偿还的情况下将公司出售给另一家私募股权公司，那么通常贷款会立即到期，因此新的私募股权买家必须向其贷款人融资，以支付旧私募股权所有者的款项，然后再偿还给其贷款人。就像抵押贷款一样，如果旧所有者在低利率和宽松信贷期间获得贷款，而新买家必须在高利率和更困难的信贷期间获得贷款，一些交易就无法完成：公司对于拥有廉价债务的旧所有者而言价值更高，而对于拥有昂贵债务的新所有者而言价值更低。

The analogy is not perfect: A lot of the private-equity loans will be floating-rate, so the difference is not so much “3% rates versus 7% rates” as it is “relatively easy credit versus more nervous credit.” And “the loans come due immediately” can mean less “the loans come due immediately” and more “the loans *technically* come due immediately, which gives the lenders negotiating leverage to extract fees and concessions for agreeing to roll them over for the new buyer.” But the basic idea is that if private-equity buyout loans were assumable, then more deals would get done, because buyers wouldn’t have to worry about going out and getting new loans in tougher credit environments.
类比并不完美：很多私募股权贷款将是浮动利率，所以不是“3%利率对比7%利率”这么简单，而是“相对容易的信贷对比更紧张的信贷”。而“贷款立即到期”可能意味着不是“贷款立即到期”，而更多是“技术上贷款立即到期，这给了贷款人谈判杠杆，以便为同意为新买家展期而收取费用和让步。”但基本观点是，如果私募股权收购贷款是可转让的，那么会有更多交易达成，因为买家不必担心在信贷环境更为严格时去寻找新贷款。

Here are Bloomberg’s John Sage and Ellen Schneider on [assumable private equity loans](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-01-04/private-credit-helps-pe-peers-by-making-deal-financing-portable):
这里是彭博社的约翰·塞奇和埃伦·施耐德谈论可转让的私募股权贷款：

> Private equity firms, eager to sell debt-laden businesses, are finding private credit firms increasingly willing to keep outstanding loans intact, even for companies that may soon have new owners.
> 私募股权公司急于出售负债累累的企业，发现私人信贷公司越来越愿意保持未偿贷款不变，即使对于可能很快会有新业主的公司也是如此。
>
> The trend, known as portability, describes loans that remain essentially unchanged when a company gets new ownership. It carries rewards and risks for businesses and especially for lenders. Usually a change of control would allow lenders to renegotiate terms to cover potential risks from a new parent, such as different plans for growth or profitability of a business.
> 所谓的可携性趋势描述了在公司更换所有权时基本保持不变的贷款。这对企业和尤其是放贷人来说既带来了回报，也带来了风险。通常情况下，控制权的变更会让放贷人重新协商条款，以覆盖新母公司可能带来的风险，比如不同的业务增长或盈利计划。
>
> After two years of rising interest costs hindering asset sales, owners are seizing on recent rate stability to push for portability to get deals done. Keeping the existing loan package in place removes the need for any new buyer to find financing, making the purchase a lot more alluring.
> 经过两年来不断上涨的利息成本阻碍资产销售，业主们正在抓住最近的利率稳定来推动可携性，以完成交易。保持现有的贷款方案不变消除了任何新买家寻找融资的需要，使购买变得更具吸引力。
>
> “The market has pivoted to include a portability feature,” said Bill Eckmann, head of principal finance in the Americas and senior managing director at Macquarie Capital. “We’re seeing more of this because sponsors are looking at near-term maturities and thinking about their exits.”
> “市场已经转向包含可携带性功能，”Macquarie Capital美洲首席融资主管兼高级董事比尔·埃克曼说。“我们看到更多这样的情况，因为赞助商正在关注近期到期和考虑退出。”
>
> For direct lenders facing rising competition in a market that’s tripled to $1.6 trillion since 2015, portability provisions allow them to stay invested in assets they’ve already vetted and endorsed.
> 对于直接放贷人来说，面临着自2015年以来增长三倍至1.6万亿美元的市场竞争，可携带性条款使他们能够继续投资已经审查和认可的资产。
>
> “If you’ve found an attractive business then you may be willing to let the debt travel to another owner,” said Jon Bock, senior managing director at Blackstone Credit. “From a self-selection standpoint, this is an opportunity for managers to extend the lives of the loan.”
> “如果你找到了一家有吸引力的企业，那么你可能愿意让债务转移到另一位所有者身上，”Blackstone Credit的高级董事总经理乔恩·博克说道。“从自我选择的角度来看，这是一个让管理者延长贷款寿命的机会。”

Part of the pitch for private credit, as an alternative to traditional syndicated loans, has been along the lines of “if you borrow from a private credit firm instead of from a syndicate of banks and hedge funds and collateralized loan obligations, your lender will be one person with whom you have a good relationship, and if things change in your business you can call her up and discuss things rationally instead of having to go get waivers from a dozen anonymous CLOs.” You might think that this pitch would argue against automatic portability: “Don’t worry about it,” the private-credit firm could tell its borrowers, “if you want to sell, we will be very reasonable about rolling over the loans, you just need to call us and talk about it.” But, no, the borrowers want portability.
私人信贷的一部分宣传内容是，作为传统银团贷款的替代方案，大致是“如果您从私人信贷公司借款，而不是从一家银行和对冲基金以及抵押贷款义务人组成的银团，您的贷款人将是一个您有良好关系的人，如果您的业务发生变化，您可以给她打电话，理性地讨论事情，而不是不得不从十几个匿名的CLO那里获得豁免。”您可能会认为这种宣传会反对自动可携性：“别担心，”私人信贷公司可以告诉其借款人，“如果您想出售，我们将非常合理地延续贷款，您只需要给我们打电话并讨论一下。”但是，借款人想要可携性。

### Money for votes 拉票贿赂

Someone [sent this to me](https://www.svegroup.com/) and I can’t not share it with you:
有人把这个发给我，我忍不住要和你分享：

> Shareholder Vote Exchange enables investors to trade shareholder voting rights. Passive investors can sell their votes to raise returns and provide additional yield. Reinvesting your earnings can also boost your portfolio's long-term growth rate. ...
> 股东投票交易使投资者能够交易股东投票权。被动投资者可以出售他们的选票以提高回报并提供额外收益。重新投资您的收益也可以提高您的投资组合的长期增长率。...
>
> Achieve your governance or strategic initiatives by acquiring proxy votes on Shareholder Vote Exchange. Leveraging proxies is a cost-effective way to drive change and maximize capital efficiency while also rewarding other shareholders.
> 通过在股东投票交易所获取代理投票，实现您的治理或战略计划。利用代理投票是推动变革、最大化资本效率的经济有效方式，同时也能奖励其他股东。

As a former corporate equity derivatives structurer I have, over the years, thought about ways to separate shareholder voting from economic ownership, and to trade the votes separately. One obvious set of solutions is: You are an activist hedge fund, you buy 10 million shares of stock (which gives you the votes and the economic ownership), and then you sell 9 million shares through a derivative (which reduces your economic ownership but *not*, generally, your voting rights). So, like, buy 10 million shares in the cash market, and then write a 9-million-share total return swap or put/call combo or whatever. Then you own 1 million shares economically, but you have 10 million votes.
作为一名前公司权益衍生品结构师，多年来我一直在思考如何将股东投票权与经济所有权分开，并分开交易这些投票权。一种明显的解决方案是：你是一家激进对冲基金，你购买了1000万股股票（这样你就拥有了投票权和经济所有权），然后通过衍生品卖出了900万股（这样可以减少你的经济所有权，但通常不会影响你的投票权）。所以，比如，在现金市场购买1000万股，然后写入900万股的总回报掉期或认购/认沽组合或其他方式。然后你在经济上拥有100万股，但你有1000万票。

I think that this occasionally happens, but my impression is that US activist hedge funds are [more likely to do the *opposite*](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-12-16/the-sec-has-big-plans), acquiring economic exposure to more shares than they actually own. (By *buying* total return swaps or call options or whatever.) This is partly for regulatory reasons (buying a bunch of actual shares triggers [disclosure](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2022-03-24/the-sec-wants-to-stop-activism) and [antitrust](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2015-08-25/dan-loeb-s-hedge-fund-filed-a-form-late) obligations that derivatives can avoid) and partly for [leverage reasons](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-07-29/archegos-was-too-busy-for-margin-calls) (buying a lot of shares for cash takes a lot of cash, while buying/selling with derivatives doesn’t take/generate as much cash.) If you are going to spend money on research and lawyers and proxy fights to do an activist campaign, you want a lot of economic exposure, not just a lot of votes.
我认为这种情况偶尔会发生，但我的印象是美国的激进对冲基金更有可能做相反的事情，获得比他们实际拥有的股份更多的经济敞口。（通过购买总回报掉期或认购期权或其他方式。）这在一定程度上是出于监管原因（购买大量实际股份会触发披露和反垄断义务，而衍生品可以避免）和部分是出于杠杆原因（用现金购买大量股份需要大量现金，而用衍生品买卖则不需要/产生那么多现金。）如果你要花钱进行研究、请律师和进行代理战争来进行激进活动，你希望获得很多经济敞口，而不仅仅是很多选票。

There is another set of quasi-solutions around [stock lending](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-01-19/maybe-the-index-funds-don-t-vote): If you do not value shareholder voting at all, you can just lend out your shares to short sellers and not recall them for votes, which is a way of exchanging your voting rights (which you don’t care about) for money (in the form of stock lending fees).
在股票借出方面还有另一组准解决方案：如果你根本不重视股东投票权，你可以将股票借给做空者，而不召回它们进行投票，这是一种将你的投票权（你不在乎的）换成金钱（以股票借出费形式）的方式。

But I guess the most obvious solution is just, like, go around paying retail shareholders for their votes? With some intermediary to aggregate them and take care of the mechanics?7 The Shareholder Vote Exchange people are not wrong that, if someone is willing to pay you $5 to control all your votes on all your shares for all of this proxy season, you should take the money, because $5 is more than zero and voting your shares is (1) worthless to you and (2) surprisingly annoying. Retail investors stereotypically [don’t vote](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2023-02-01/amc-has-some-clever-apes), and that is [normally rational](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2022-09-21/don-t-read-the-proxy-statement), so paying them any amount of money for their votes seems like a win for them.
但我想最明显的解决方案就是，比如，给零售股东支付他们的选票？通过一些中介来汇总它们并处理相关机制？股东投票交易所的人们并没有错，如果有人愿意支付你5美元来控制你在本次代理季节的所有股份的所有选票，你应该接受这笔钱，因为5美元总比零多，而且投票你的股份（1）对你毫无价值，（2）令人惊讶地烦人。零售投资者通常不投票，这通常是理性的，因此为他们的选票支付任何金额似乎对他们有利。

On the other hand, who would pay? If it is irrational to vote your own shares, it is even more irrational to pay to vote someone else’s, virtually all of the time. You could imagine some sorts of activist campaigns in which it would be expressively valuable to buy some votes: If you submitted a nonbinding shareholder proposal asking some company to have more, or less, diversity, then presumably you care about sending a message, and you might feel good about spending some of your own money to buy votes to make your proposal look more popular.
另一方面，谁会付钱呢？如果投票自己的股份是不理智的，那么几乎所有情况下为了投票别人的股份而付费更加不理智。你可以想象一些激进活动中会有一些明确有价值的情况：如果你提交了一个非约束性的股东提案，要求某家公司增加或减少多样性，那么你显然关心传递信息，你可能会乐意花一些自己的钱来购买选票，使你的提案看起来更受欢迎。

But surely the real high-dollar case for buying shareholder votes is in *contested proxy fights*, where some activist investor has millions of dollars on the line and wants to get its own slate of directors elected. (Also in contested takeovers.) And there I just have trouble imagining big activist shareholders buying votes in proxy fights this way, in part because the disclosure and reputational issues seem like they’d be a mess. The distinguishing feature of proxy fights and hostile takeovers is that everyone sues everyone else about everything, and also “bedbugs” them to the US Securities and Exchange Commission, calling up the SEC to say “hey did you notice our opponents doing something illegal?” Can you imagine the hay that some corporate managers would make out of an evil activist hedge fund *buying votes*? 
但肯定真正高价值的购买股东投票案例是在有争议的代理人斗争中，一些激进投资者在其中投入数百万美元，并希望选举自己的董事会成员。（同样适用于有争议的收购。）在这种情况下，我很难想象大型激进投资者会以这种方式购买代理人斗争中的选票，部分原因是披露和声誉问题似乎会变得一团糟。代理人斗争和敌意收购的区别在于，每个人都会就任何事情起诉其他人，还会向美国证券交易委员会“告密”，打电话给SEC说“嘿，你注意到我们的对手在做违法的事情了吗？”你能想象一些公司经理会如何利用邪恶的激进对冲基金购买选票的情况吗？

And in fact Shareholder Vote Exchange’s [current list of auctions](https://www.svegroup.com/auction) seems to be headed by pretty routine annual meetings for companies like Visa Inc. and Intuit Inc., none of which have proxy fights ongoing. Still, fun idea!
事实上，股东投票交易所目前的拍卖清单似乎主要是一些像Visa Inc.和Intuit Inc.这样的公司的例行年度会议，其中没有进行中的代理人争夺。不过，这个想法很有趣！

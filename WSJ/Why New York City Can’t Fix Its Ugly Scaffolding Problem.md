# Why New York City Can’t Fix Its Ugly Scaffolding Problem

## Thanks to archaic laws, the Big Apple is filled with sidewalk sheds that stay for years 由于陈旧的法律，大苹果城充斥着长年不散的人行道棚

By 

[Erin Ailworth](https://www.wsj.com/news/author/erin-ailworth)



 由艾琳·艾尔沃思报道 | Photographs and video by Jonah Markowitz for The Wall Street Journal 
照片和视频由《华尔街日报》的乔纳·马科维茨拍摄

Feb. 18, 2024 at 5:30 am ET
2024年2月18日上午5:30 ET

![Scaffolding covers the sidewalks near buildings at the intersection of West End Avenue and West 88th Street in Manhattan.](https://images.wsj.net/im-925562/?width=1278&size=1)

NEW YORK—Anne “LaVerne” Gaither teared up when workers dismantled the hunter-green plywood and metal scaffolding that for 21 years obscured the entrance of her Harlem apartment building.
纽约-当工人拆除了21年来遮挡着她哈莱姆公寓大楼入口的猎绿色胶合板和金属脚手架时，安妮“拉维恩”盖瑟感动得流下了眼泪。

The 220-foot-long structure, known here as a [sidewalk shed](https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB124812702555366417?mod=article_inline), was New York City’s oldest existing shed until it came down in December. It was originally built in 2002 to protect pedestrians during required repairs to the neo-Georgian facade of 409 Edgecombe Ave., a century-old landmark once home to luminaries such as W.E.B. DuBois and [Thurgood Marshall](https://www.wsj.com/us-news/education/historically-black-colleges-land-124-million-donation-to-boost-enrollment-graduation-rates-8e639878?mod=article_inline). 
这座长220英尺的结构，在这里被称为人行道棚，直到去年12月才被拆除，它是纽约市现存最古老的棚子。它最初建于2002年，用于保护行人在对409 Edgecombe Ave.的新格鲁吉亚式立面进行必要修复期间。这座百年历史的地标曾是W.E.B. DuBois和Thurgood Marshall等名人的故居。

Gaither, 93 years old, used to keep bottles of water for the workers on the wooden platform outside her second-story window. “My tears were thanks for being alive to see it come down,” she said.
盖瑟，93岁，过去常在她二楼窗外的木平台上为工人们准备瓶装水。她说：“我流的泪是因为活着看到它倒塌而感激。”

A few weeks later, damage caused by a deadly fire forced the rise of a new shed.
几周后，一场致命火灾造成的损害迫使新棚屋的兴起。

The building’s shed saga is an extreme example of the staying power of New York City’s scaffolding, whose ubiquity spurred some to joke that it is the Big Apple’s official tree. More than 8,300 sidewalk sheds currently enshroud some 360 miles of the city’s sidewalks, according to permit data from the New York City Department of Buildings. (Those numbers encompass active permits without taking into account whether a property owner changed vendors or briefly removed, and then replaced, scaffolding under a new permit.) Around 300 of those sheds are more than five years old.
建筑物的棚屋传奇是纽约市脚手架持久力的一个极端例子，其普遍存在促使一些人开玩笑说它是大苹果的官方树。根据纽约市建筑部门的许可数据，目前有超过8,300个人行道棚屋覆盖了该市约360英里的人行道（这些数字包括活跃许可证，不考虑产权所有人是否更换供应商或暂时移除然后再用新的许可证重新安装脚手架）。其中约有300个棚屋已经存在超过五年。

The building at **571 West End Ave.** in Manhattan has had a sidewalk shed out front for more than a decade. Manhattan Borough President Mark Levine identified it as one of the island’s oldest.曼哈顿西区571号西区大道的建筑物前面已经有一个人行道棚亭超过十年了。曼哈顿区总统马克·莱文将其确定为该岛上最古老的之一。

The building is one of dozens nearby with sheds, many of which also are years old.这座建筑物是附近数十座带有棚子的建筑之一，其中许多也有数年历史。

The surrounding **Central Upper West Side** has 271 in place. It is the neighborhood with the most sheds—41—that are more than three years old.周边的中上西区有271个地方。这是拥有最多棚屋的社区，有41个棚屋已经超过三年了。

The proliferation of scaffolding sheds extends across Manhattan and into other boroughs.脚手架棚的扩散遍布曼哈顿和其他行政区。

Sources: NYC Dept. of Buildings (scaffolding, as of Jan.); NYC OTI (buildings)
来源：纽约市建筑部（脚手架，截至一月）；纽约市OTI（建筑物）
Max Rust and Peter Champelli/THE WALL STREET JOURNAL
马克斯·拉斯特和彼得·钱佩利/《华尔街日报》

The buildup is the result of a cocktail of issues, including insufficient oversight by regulators; [supply bottlenecks](https://www.wsj.com/articles/new-disruptions-geopolitics-hang-over-2024-supply-chains-6fca8f1e?mod=article_inline), particularly for specialty items needed by landmark buildings; and financial struggles at low-income buildings.
积累是一系列问题的结果，包括监管机构的不足监督；供应瓶颈，尤其是地标建筑所需的特殊物品；以及低收入建筑物的财务困境。

Perhaps the biggest factor, say many officials—including Mayor Eric Adams—is the set of arcane local rules requiring sidewalk sheds for pedestrian protection during construction and demolition, facade work, and other exterior maintenance. The laws stem from the death of a Barnard College student killed by a falling piece of masonry in 1979.
许多官员（包括市长埃里克·亚当斯）表示，可能最大的因素是一套古老的地方规定，要求在建筑和拆除、外墙工程和其他外部维护期间设置人行道棚以保护行人。这些法律源于1979年一名巴纳德学院学生被掉落的砖石击中身亡的事件。

The rules, particularly those governing facade inspections and repairs, encourage property owners to leave sheds up instead of completing critical and often costly work, the Adams administration said. 
规定，尤其是关于外立面检查和修复的规定，鼓励物业所有者保留棚屋，而不是完成关键且常常昂贵的工作，亚当斯政府表示。

With all possible fines levied, it can cost about $100,000 a year to install and maintain a shed. Facade repairs, meanwhile, can cost millions.
安装和维护一个小屋可能需要支付高达10万美元的费用。而外墙修复可能需要数百万美元。

“Too many sheds have become permanent fixtures of our landscape,” Adams said in December**,** while celebrating the removal of the 21-year-old shed at 409 Edgecombe.
“亚当斯在12月份表示，太多的棚屋已经成为我们景观中的永久设施，”他在庆祝位于Edgecombe 409号的21年历史的棚屋被拆除时说道。

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-925559?size=1.4&height=900)







Anne 'LaVerne' Gaither of 409 Edgecombe Ave.
409 Edgecombe Ave.的安妮·拉维恩·盖瑟

Nikki Berryman, president of 409 Edgecombe’s board, said a lightning strike that hit the building during superstorm Sandy, just as workers finished more than $1 million in repairs, extended the shed’s presence. Raising money for the new repairs took the low-income co-op a lot of time, as did replacing terracotta elements with fiberglass.
尼基·贝里曼是409 Edgecombe的董事会主席，她表示，在超级飓风桑迪期间，一次雷击打中了这座建筑，正好在工人完成了超过100万美元的修复工作后，延长了棚子的存在。为新的修复筹集资金花费了这个低收入合作社很多时间，替换陶瓷元素为玻璃纤维也是如此。

“It was very daunting,” Berryman said.
“这非常令人畏惧，”贝里曼说。

According to city data, roughly 40% of sheds proliferate when a facade inspection—required every five years for buildings higher than six stories—finds unsafe conditions needing repair. New construction, major alterations and demolition, as well as maintenance on smaller buildings, account for the other 60%.
根据城市数据，大约40%的棚屋在每五年一次的外立面检查中繁殖，这是对高于六层的建筑物所要求的，发现需要修复的不安全条件。新建筑、重大改建和拆除，以及对较小建筑物的维护占了其余的60%。

Critics say the facade inspection and safety rules are well-meaning, but crafted in a rigid way that is out of step with how buildings age. Newer glass and steel skyscrapers are treated the same way as decades-old brick and terracotta buildings, for instance. 
批评者表示，外墙检查和安全规定虽然出于善意，但制定方式过于僵化，与建筑物老化的实际情况不符。例如，较新的玻璃和钢铁摩天大楼与几十年前的砖石和陶瓷建筑物被同等对待。

Property owners are also required to erect sidewalk sheds to protect walkways within a distance of half the height of the building. In a city full of skyscrapers, that has led to sheds in some nonsensical places just to comply with the letter of the law, said Stephen Smith, executive director of the Center for Building in North America, a nonprofit that does research on building codes and construction policies.
物业所有者还必须建立人行道棚以保护建筑物高度一半范围内的人行道。在一个充满摩天大楼的城市里，这导致了一些毫无意义的地方建立了棚子，只是为了遵守法律的字面要求，北美建筑中心的执行主任斯蒂芬·史密斯说道。该非营利组织致力于研究建筑法规和建筑政策。

“This isn’t how things fall in real life—this is not how gravity works,” Smith said. “Obviously, what’s going on here is the city is on a hair trigger when it comes to ordering these things to be put up.”
“这不是现实生活中物体掉落的方式 - 这不是重力的工作方式，”史密斯说。“显然，这里发生的是，当城市下令安装这些东西时，它们处于非常敏感的状态。”

A 2021 audit of New York City sidewalk sheds by the state comptroller found the city’s Department of Buildings “needs to be more proactive in ensuring that owners and other responsible parties comply with relevant codes, laws, and rules pertaining to the timely permitting, installation, maintenance, and removal of sheds.” A 2023 follow-up found the city had made limited progress implementing the audit’s recommendations**,** a notion the city disagrees with.
2021年纽约市州审计员对人行道棚棚架进行的审计发现，该市建筑部门“需要更加积极主动地确保业主和其他责任方遵守与棚架的及时许可、安装、维护和拆除相关的法规、法律和规定。”2023年的跟进审计发现，该市在实施审计建议方面取得了有限的进展，但该市对此持不同意见。

Meera Joshi, the deputy mayor for operations, said Adams is working on reforms, such as speeding up the process for the city to take on capital projects. The city of New York, itself a large-property owner, struggles with lingering sheds when its own buildings need facade work. 
美拉·乔希，运营副市长表示，亚当斯正在推动改革，例如加快城市承担资本项目的流程。纽约市本身是大型房地产所有者，但在自己的建筑需要外立面工作时，仍然面临着长期的棚棚问题。

“We know we will never get rid of scaffolding,” she said. “We’ll do everything we can do to minimize it.”
“我们知道我们永远无法摆脱脚手架，”她说。“我们将尽一切努力来将其最小化。”

The Department of Buildings recently has stepped up its efforts to take building owners to court over longstanding sheds. 
建筑部门最近加大了对长期存在的棚屋问题追究建筑业主责任的努力。

The scaffolding scourge is worst in Manhattan, where it is almost impossible to walk any distance without encountering one of the borough’s roughly 3,800 sidewalk sheds, including those along famed thoroughfares such as Broadway and Fifth Avenue.
脚手架的问题在曼哈顿最为严重，几乎不可能在这个区域走上一段距离而不遇到其中大约3800个的人行道棚，包括那些位于著名街道如百老汇和第五大道的脚手架。

STATEN ISLAND 斯塔滕岛

Manhattan Borough President Mark Levine, who recently called out some of the oldest sheds with [mock awards](https://www.instagram.com/p/C2ztqSkO17l/?mc_cid=4584b75d6e&mc_eid=f65e1ef4b), wants the city to lengthen the timespan between required facade inspections in some cases and provide low-interest loans for financially strapped buildings, among other reforms. Adams said he generally agrees with the ideas. 
曼哈顿区区长马克·莱文最近对一些最古老的棚屋进行了嘲讽性评选，并希望城市在某些情况下延长外墙检查的时间间隔，并为财政困难的建筑物提供低利贷款等改革措施。亚当斯表示他基本上同意这些想法。

Levine said the scope of the problem was apparent in April 2023, when news cameras tried to catch a glimpse of former President Donald Trump arriving for an [arraignment in lower Manhattan](https://twitter.com/MarkLevineNYC/status/1643580501125464066).
莱文表示，问题的范围在2023年4月显而易见，当新闻摄像机试图捕捉到前总统唐纳德·特朗普在曼哈顿下城出庭时。

“The entire building was covered in scaffolding, and we could barely see him when he got out of the car,” Levine said. “It has kind of defined our aesthetic.”
整个建筑物都被脚手架覆盖着，当他下车时我们几乎看不见他，”Levine说。“这在某种程度上定义了我们的审美。”

Adams has called for potential penalties of up to $6,000 monthly for failing to do work integral to removing a shed.
亚当斯呼吁对未能完成与拆除棚屋相关的工作可能处以每月高达6000美元的罚款。

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-925561?width=1260&height=841)

New Yorkers both human and canine have grown accustomed to scaffolding overhead.
纽约人，无论是人类还是狗，都已经习惯了头顶上的脚手架。

Some New Yorkers say they have lived so long surrounded by scaffolding that they doubt the current proposals will lead to change.
一些纽约人表示，他们生活在脚手架的环绕中已经很久了，他们对目前的提议是否能带来改变表示怀疑。

In 2021, residents at 51 West 86th St. threw a facetious anniversary party for the sidewalk shed that has loomed, with little interruption, in front of their landmark apartment building for more than 15 years. Information from the city shows that shed permits cover much of that span.
2021年，位于86街西51号的居民为他们的地标公寓楼前已经存在了15多年的人行道棚举办了一个滑稽的周年派对。城市提供的信息显示，棚子的许可证覆盖了大部分时间。

A resident in a unit overlooking one of the scaffolding’s planks said it attracts unwanted guests: noisy, messy pigeons.
一个住在俯瞰脚手架之一的单元的居民说，它吸引了不受欢迎的客人：吵闹、凌乱的鸽子。

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-925563?width=700&height=467)

Naomi Johnson on a roof at Howard Houses, a 10-building NYCHA complex in Brooklyn. 
纽约布鲁克林的霍华德公寓，Naomi Johnson站在屋顶上。

“The procreating, the feeding of the babies, the squawking,” the resident said. “We just keep the windows closed and the music on.”
“繁殖、喂养幼崽、嘎嘎叫”，居民说道。“我们只是把窗户关上，开着音乐。”

The city in 2022 sued the building’s owner, Jacob Weinreb, of Weinreb Management, for failing to correct “hazardous facade defects” at 11 properties in the company’s portfolio. The case is continuing.
该城市在2022年起诉了建筑物的所有者Jacob Weinreb，他是Weinreb Management的负责人，因为该公司投资组合中的11个物业存在“危险的外墙缺陷”，但未能进行修复。此案仍在进行中。

Weinreb said his company has been actively doing facade work, and some sheds are already down.
温雷布表示，他的公司一直在积极进行外立面工作，一些棚子已经拆除。

A number of scaffolds are at New York City Housing Authority complexes, such as Howard Houses, a sprawling campus with 10 apartment buildings—each with scaffolding several years old—in Brooklyn’s Brownsville neighborhood.
一些脚手架位于纽约市住房局的综合体内，比如霍华德公寓，这是一个庞大的校园，位于布鲁克林的布朗斯维尔社区，有10栋公寓楼，每栋楼都有几年历史的脚手架。

Naomi Johnson, president of the complex’s tenants association, said she objected when the city decorated some of the sheds there with art.
纳奥米·约翰逊，该综合体租户协会的主席，表示当城市将一些棚屋装饰成艺术品时，她提出了反对。

“I want them down,” she said, adding that the city told her some could be down soon. “I don’t need them to be beautified.”
“我希望它们被拆除，”她说道，还补充说城市告诉她有些可能很快就会被拆除。“我不需要它们被美化。”

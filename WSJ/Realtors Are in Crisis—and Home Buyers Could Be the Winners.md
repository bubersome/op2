# Realtors Are in Crisis—and Home Buyers Could Be the Winners

## A wave of lawsuits over fees paid to agents has put the giant trade association on the defense; ‘it got arrogant’

By 

[Laura Kusisto](https://www.wsj.com/news/author/laura-kusisto)



 and 

[Nicole Friedman](https://www.wsj.com/news/author/nicole-friedman)



Feb. 16, 2024 at 5:30 am ET

![Single-family homes in Carpinteria, Calif.](https://images.wsj.net/im-926309/?width=1278&size=1)

The National Association of Realtors appeared to hit rock bottom in October when a Kansas City jury [delivered a $1.8 billion verdict](https://www.wsj.com/real-estate/jury-finds-realtors-conspired-to-keep-commissions-high-awards-nearly-1-8-billion-in-damages-b26f9c2f), finding that the industry kept home-sales fees artificially high. But the existential threat to the powerful trade group was about to get worse.

Brokerage executives are pressuring NAR to settle the legal claims nationwide, worried that more large verdicts could push residential firms and local real-estate associations into bankruptcy. They complain that NAR failed to convey the gravity of the threat.

“There has been consistently an arrogant attitude of the senior officers” of the association, said Dave Liniger, chairman of brokerage franchiser Re/Max Holdings. “It just seemed to me that it was just like a solid wall of, ‘It’s our way, and we’re going to keep it that way, and we don’t care.’” Re/Max was also a defendant in the Kansas City lawsuit, but it settled before the trial.
经纪公司 Re/Max Holdings 的董事长戴夫·莱尼格表示，协会的“高级官员一直表现出傲慢的态度”。“在我看来，这就像一堵坚固的墙，‘这是我们的方式，我们将继续保持这种方式，我们不在乎。’”Re/Max 也是堪萨斯城诉讼中的被告，但在审判前已达成和解。

A NAR spokeswoman said the organization has always been committed to working with the industry to improve its policies and resolve the legal threats. 
全国房地产经纪人协会的一位发言人表示，该组织一直致力于与业界合作，以改善其政策并解决法律威胁。

The Missouri jury found that the powerful trade organization and two brokerages had conspired to keep fees paid to buyers’ agents high. Long before that fiasco, some real-estate executives had urged NAR to change the decades-old commission structure, but the organization refused.
密苏里州陪审团认定，这个强大的行业组织和两家经纪公司密谋将支付给买方代理的费用保持在高位。早在这次惨败之前，一些房地产高管就敦促全国房地产经纪人协会改变沿用数十年的佣金结构，但该组织拒绝了。

The industry now [appears headed for significant changes](https://www.wsj.com/finance/investing/the-way-you-pay-to-buy-or-sell-a-home-is-about-to-change-4e1a4fbc) in how buyers’ agents are paid, a system that NAR has fought for decades to protect. One possibility is that sellers would no longer decide upfront how much buyers’ agents get paid, giving buyers more power to negotiate. More buyers might opt to buy homes without using agents at all, which could save them money on commissions but might put less sophisticated buyers at a disadvantage. 
该行业现在似乎正朝着买方代理人报酬方式发生重大变化的方向发展，而 NAR 几十年来一直为保护这一体系而斗争。一种可能性是，卖方不再预先决定买方代理人获得的报酬，从而赋予买方更大的谈判权力。更多买家可能会选择在不使用代理人的情况下购买房屋，这可以为他们节省佣金，但可能会让不太精明的买方处于不利地位。

The Chicago headquarters of the National Association of Realtors. Members pay hundreds of dollars a year in dues to join their national, state and local Realtor associations. Photo: Danielle A. Scruggs/The Wall Street Journal
全国房地产经纪人协会的芝加哥总部。会员每年支付数百美元的会费以加入其全国、州和地方房地产经纪人协会。照片：华尔街日报的丹妮尔·A·斯克鲁格斯

Consumer advocates hope that changes to NAR’s rules would lower commissions, making home purchases a little more affordable. The typical commission—5% to 6% of the purchase price, split between the buyer’s and seller’s representatives—[is one of the highest in the world](https://www.wsj.com/personal-finance/real-estate-buying-home-charts-6dc40caa).
消费者倡导者希望对 NAR 的规则进行更改，以降低佣金，使购房变得更实惠一些。典型的佣金——5% 到 6% 的购买价格，在买方和卖方代表之间分配——是世界上最高的佣金之一。

For the industry, such changes could prove painful. If fewer buyers use agents, that could push many agents out of the industry, reducing NAR’s size and lobbying power. And if real-estate commissions go down, that would mean less revenue for real-estate brokerages.
对于该行业来说，此类变化可能会带来痛苦。如果使用代理的买家减少，可能会迫使许多代理退出该行业，从而减少 NAR 的规模和游说能力。如果房地产佣金下降，则意味着房地产经纪公司的收入会减少。

NAR has 1.5 million members and boasts that it is the largest trade organization in the nation. It has total assets of more than $1 billion. Yet its future has rarely looked more uncertain. 
NAR 拥有 150 万名会员，并自称是全国最大的行业组织。其总资产超过 10 亿美元。然而，它的未来却很少如此不确定。

“NAR is at the forefront of issues affecting the lives of millions of people,” said Nykia Wright, the organization’s interim chief executive, in a written statement. “We have a profound obligation when navigating complex litigation that has the potential to affect not just our industry but the entire American economy. While some are focused on the past, we are looking to the future.”
该组织的临时首席执行官 Nykia Wright 在一份书面声明中表示：“NAR 处于影响数百万人生计的问题的最前沿。”“在处理可能不仅影响我们行业而且影响整个美国经济的复杂诉讼时，我们负有深远的义务。虽然有些人关注过去，但我们着眼于未来。”

Over its 116-year history, NAR has repeatedly fended off lawsuits and Justice Department inquiries about industry practices. Its legal maneuvering and political clout enabled NAR to maintain a tight grip over the residential real-estate business, shaping how Americans buy and sell homes and the costs associated with those transactions. (News Corp, owner of The Wall Street Journal, operates Realtor.com under license from NAR.)
在 116 年的历史中，NAR 已多次抵御了针对行业惯例的诉讼和司法部调查。其法律策略和政治影响力使 NAR 能够牢牢掌控住宅房地产业务，塑造美国人买卖房屋的方式以及与这些交易相关的成本。（新闻集团，华尔街日报的所有者，在获得 NAR 许可的情况下运营 Realtor.com。）

NAR’s members, the only real-estate agents who can call themselves Realtors, must pay hundreds of dollars a year in dues to join their national, state and local Realtor associations.
NAR 的成员是唯一可以称自己为房地产经纪人的房地产经纪人，他们必须每年支付数百美元的会费才能加入其全国、州和地方房地产经纪人协会。

NAR is “this massive hulking institution that lives on lots of teeny members’ payments. It’s like a whale that is sustained on krill, and this gave it a certain institutional durability,” said Brian Boero, chief executive of 1000watt, a residential real-estate strategy consulting firm. “It got big, it got flabby, it got arrogant and it got complacent.” 
NAR 是“一个庞大的庞然大物，靠许多小成员的会费维持生计。它就像一条靠磷虾为生的鲸鱼，这给了它一定的机构持久力，”1000watt 住宅房地产战略咨询公司的首席执行官布莱恩·博罗说。“它变得庞大，变得松弛，变得傲慢自大，变得自满。”

The precursor to the modern organization was founded in 1908. Early on, the association helped professionalize an industry in which agents were afraid to list properties publicly lest other agents steal their clients by offering lower rates. 
现代组织的前身成立于 1908 年。早期，该协会帮助专业化了一个行业，在这个行业中，代理商害怕公开列出房产，以免其他代理商以较低的价格窃取他们的客户。

Local associations, with support from the national organization, began distributing mandatory fee schedules. Undercutting those rates was considered unethical. In 1950, the Supreme Court said that practice was illegal, and the fee schedules became recommended. 
在国家组织的支持下，地方协会开始分发强制性费用表。低于这些费率被认为是不道德的。1950 年，最高法院表示这种做法是非法的，费用表成为推荐性的。

By the 1970s, the industry had largely gotten rid of recommended fees after facing lawsuits, Justice Department investigations and even criminal charges against real-estate firms for conspiring to fix prices.
到了 20 世纪 70 年代，在面临诉讼、司法部调查甚至针对房地产公司串通定价的刑事指控后，该行业已基本取消了建议费用。

Under NAR’s current commission structure, which dates to the 1990s, most sellers listing properties offer to pay the buyers’ representatives. 
根据 NAR 目前的佣金结构（可追溯到 20 世纪 90 年代），大多数出售房产的卖家都会提供支付买方代表的费用。

Plaintiffs and government regulators have argued this system keeps commissions high by preventing buyers from negotiating fees with their own agents and allowing buyers’ agents to steer clients away from homes that offer lower commissions. The association says the current commission structure helps first-time buyers who can’t afford to pay an agent out of pocket.
原告和政府监管机构认为，该系统通过阻止买家与自己的代理协商费用并允许买家的代理将客户引导至提供较低佣金的房屋，从而使佣金保持在高位。该协会表示，目前的佣金结构有助于那些无力支付代理费用的首次购房者。

In 2006, real-estate listing firm 

[Zillow](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/Z)

began posting online information about homes on the market, attracting more than a million visitors in the first few days. Although the rise of online shopping has led to lower fees for travel agents and stockbrokers, real-estate agent commissions have risen because home prices have climbed. 
2006 年，房地产信息公司 Zillow 开始在网上发布待售房屋的信息，在最初的几天里吸引了超过一百万的访问者。尽管网上购物的兴起导致旅行社和股票经纪人的费用降低，但由于房价上涨，房地产经纪人的佣金却有所上升。



Commissions paid to buyer representatives rose 32% from 2013 to 2020, adjusted for inflation, according to an expert witness report in a pending class-action lawsuit in Chicago that examined 20 regions. 
根据芝加哥一项针对 20 个地区的待决集体诉讼中的专家证人报告，经通胀调整后，支付给买方代表的佣金从 2013 年到 2020 年增长了 32%。

Doug Miller, a Minnesota lawyer with a one-man firm, began the work that would culminate in the crushing Kansas City verdict. 
道格·米勒，明尼苏达州的一名单人律师事务所的律师，开始了这项工作，最终导致了堪萨斯城判决的粉碎。

When he was in law school in the 1980s, Miller worked as a Realtor in Wisconsin. He said he concluded that the industry wasn’t working in the best interest of consumers. “It all involved betrayal,” he said.
20 世纪 80 年代，米勒在法学院学习期间，曾在威斯康星州担任房地产经纪人。他说，他得出结论，该行业并未维护消费者的最大利益。“这完全是背叛，”他说。

In the 1990s, Miller said, he visited the offices of Minnesota lawmakers to try to talk them out of supporting legislation that the real-estate industry was pushing. The lawmakers, he said, were wearing Realtor pins. 
米勒说，在 20 世纪 90 年代，他走访了明尼苏达州议员的办公室，试图劝说他们不要支持房地产行业正在推动的立法。他说，这些议员都佩戴着房地产经纪人徽章。

About five years ago, Miller [approached a law firm, Cohen Milstein](https://www.wsj.com/real-estate/how-the-1-8-billion-real-estate-commissions-lawsuit-came-to-be-106433d1), with an outline for a lawsuit that would target NAR’s rule mandating how buyer agents’ commissions are paid. Drawing on Miller’s research, the firm helped file the first major antitrust class action, in Chicago. 
大约五年前，米勒找到一家律师事务所科恩·米尔斯坦，概述了一项针对 NAR 规则的诉讼，该规则规定买方代理佣金的支付方式。该事务所利用米勒的研究，帮助在芝加哥提起首例重大反垄断集体诉讼。

About a month later, another law firm filed a similar suit in Kansas City, which wound up going to trial first. 
大约一个月后，另一家律师事务所在堪萨斯城提起了类似的诉讼，该诉讼最终进入审判程序。

As the lawsuits were progressing through the courts, the Justice Department also was looking into the industry’s practices. 
随着诉讼在法庭上进行，司法部也在调查该行业的惯例。

In 2020, under the Trump administration, the department [reached a settlement](https://www.wsj.com/articles/justice-department-takes-aim-at-realtor-rules-11605826653)with NAR requiring agents to be more transparent about commissions. 
2020年，在特朗普政府的领导下，该部门与全国房地产经纪人协会达成和解，要求经纪人对佣金更加透明。

In 2021, President Biden issued an executive order directing federal agencies to increase competition in the real-estate industry. The Justice Department has asked a federal appeals court to allow it to reopen its investigation. 
2021 年，拜登总统发布了一项行政命令，指示联邦机构增加房地产行业的竞争。司法部已要求联邦上诉法院允许其重新启动调查。

Some in the industry felt NAR had a bloated bureaucracy and was ill-prepared for a crisis. The association undertook a project a few years ago to shrink its board of directors, which had about 1,000 members. Instead, it ended up expanding it. Its presidents serve one-year terms and often seemed focused on pet projects, former NAR staffers said. 
该行业的一些人认为，全国房地产经纪人协会的官僚机构臃肿，而且没有为危机做好准备。几年前，该协会启动了一个项目，以缩小其董事会，该董事会约有 1,000 名成员。相反，它最终扩大了董事会。全国房地产经纪人协会的前工作人员表示，其主席任期为一年，而且经常专注于个人项目。

With the litigation threat looming, Kenny Parcell, a Utah Realtor who had become the association’s president in late 2022, championed a $3 million initiative that involved touring the country with a bus and motorcycle to meet with Realtors and raise money for charity. 
随着诉讼威胁迫在眉睫，2022 年底成为该协会主席的犹他州房地产经纪人肯尼·帕塞尔支持一项 300 万美元的倡议，该倡议包括乘坐公共汽车和摩托车在全国巡回，与房地产经纪人会面并为慈善机构筹集资金。

Just as the Kansas City case approached trial, the association was thrust into a leadership crisis. In August 2023, Parcell resigned after allegations of sexual harassment by several women. He has denied the allegations. 
就在堪萨斯城案件即将开庭审理之际，该协会陷入了一场领导危机。2023 年 8 月，帕塞尔在多名女性指控其性骚扰后辞职。他否认了这些指控。

His successor, Tracy Kasper, pledged to promote what she called a “welcoming, safe and respectful workplace.” Her first video message to members as president, which came out a week later, was set to music, which some industry leaders said made NAR seem out of touch.
她的继任者特蕾西·卡斯珀承诺促进她所说的“热情、安全和尊重的职场”。她作为主席向成员发送的第一条视频信息在一周后发布，并配有音乐，一些行业领导表示这使得全国房地产经纪人协会显得脱离实际。

Parcell and Kasper didn’t respond to requests for comment. A NAR spokeswoman said an “overwhelming majority” of members “have communicated their trust in what we are doing to transform the organization.” 
帕塞尔和卡斯珀没有回应置评请求。全国房地产经纪人协会的一位女发言人表示，“绝大多数”成员“已经传达了他们对我们为转型组织所做工作的信任”。

Industry leaders said they received little indication from NAR that the lawsuits posed a serious threat. The association’s chief legal officer, Katie Johnson, had said: “These cases are wrong on the facts, wrong on the law.” 
行业领导者表示，他们从全国房地产经纪人协会那里得到的关于诉讼构成严重威胁的迹象很少。该协会的首席法律官凯蒂·约翰逊曾表示：“这些案件在事实和法律上都是错误的。”

As the lawsuits gained momentum, she gave video presentations to local Realtor associations, often sitting in front of inspirational slogans such as “Hope will never be silent.” 
随着诉讼的势头增强，她在当地房地产协会进行了视频演示，经常坐在“希望永远不会沉默”等鼓舞人心的口号前。

Top real-estate executives worried about taking the class-action lawsuits in Chicago and Kansas City in front of juries. The industry executives privately urged NAR to alter its commission policy and scrap a mandate that governed how buyers’ agents get paid. Doing that, they argued, would weaken the plaintiffs’ cases. 
顶级房地产高管担心在芝加哥和堪萨斯城的陪审团面前受理集体诉讼。行业高管私下敦促全国房地产经纪人协会改变其佣金政策，并废除一项管理买方经纪人如何获得报酬的法规。他们认为，这样做会削弱原告的案件。

In February 2022, an executive at 

[Anywhere Real Estate](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/HOUS)

, one of the country’s largest real-estate brokerages, urged NAR to make that compromise. Retry  Reason



In an email to an NAR staffer, Caitlin McCrory, head of industry relations at the brokerage, wrote: “Questions about why the mandate exists are posing unnecessary complications while providing little to no discernible benefit.” 
在发给全国房地产经纪人协会工作人员凯特琳·麦克罗里的电子邮件中，该经纪公司的行业关系主管写道：“关于该授权为何存在的问题正在造成不必要的复杂性，同时几乎没有提供任何明显的好处。”

NAR leaders dismissed this advice, expressing confidence they would win and opposing any concessions, real-estate executives recall.
NAR 领导人驳回了这一建议，他们表示相信自己会获胜，并反对任何让步，房地产高管回忆道。

But just 10 days before the Kansas City trial, the association revised the rule. The lead plaintiffs’ lawyer called it a “stunning admission of guilt.” 
但就在堪萨斯城审判前 10 天，该协会修改了该规则。原告律师称之为“令人震惊的认罪”。

The NAR spokeswoman said changing the rule earlier wouldn’t have resolved the litigation, and that other industry executives opposed any change.
全国房地产经纪人协会发言人表示，早些时候改变规则并不能解决诉讼问题，而且其他行业高管反对任何改变。

Anywhere, which owns several brokerage brands including Corcoran, Coldwell Banker and Century 21, settled days before the trial, as did Re/Max. The two companies agreed to pay about $140 million combined. 
Anywhere 拥有多家经纪品牌，包括 Corcoran、Coldwell Banker 和 Century 21，在审判前几天达成和解，Re/Max 也一样。两家公司同意共支付约 1.4 亿美元。

Things finally came to a head in a Kansas City courtroom in mid-October. On the first day, the judge dismissed 10 of the roughly 80 potential jurors because, as recent home sellers, they were members of the plaintiffs’ class—a sign of how many ordinary people could be affected by the outcome.
十月中旬，堪萨斯城法庭的审判终于进入白热化阶段。第一天，法官驳回了大约 80 名潜在陪审员中的 10 人，因为他们作为最近的房屋卖家，是原告群体的一员——这表明有多少普通人可能会受到结果的影响。

Kathy Helbig-Strick, a Realtor in St. Louis, Mo., drove 3½ hours to watch part of the trial and support the industry. Kansas City plaintiffs’ lawyer Michael Ketchmark, whose elderly parents came to court each day, connected with the jury more effectively than the defense lawyers, she said. “He would get his passion going,” she said. 
密苏里州圣路易斯的房地产经纪人凯西·赫尔比格-斯特里克驱车 3 小时半来旁听部分审判并支持该行业。堪萨斯城原告律师迈克尔·凯奇马克的年迈父母每天都来法庭，她表示，他比辩护律师与陪审团建立了更有效的联系。“他会激发自己的激情，”她说。

Even after the plaintiffs settled with two major brokerages, they never got close to a settlement with NAR. “At their core they thought they were going to win,” Ketchmark said. 
即使原告与两家大型经纪公司达成和解，他们也从未与 NAR 达成和解。Ketchmark 说：“他们内心认为自己会赢。”

The jury took just over two hours to decide in favor of the plaintiffs. 
陪审团只用了两个多小时就决定支持原告。

NAR has vowed to appeal the decision, but the Chicago case could go to trial later this year. The damages in that case could total more than $40 billion, based on calculations of commissions paid in recent years. Executives in the industry are pressing NAR to settle. 
NAR 已誓言上诉，但芝加哥案件可能在今年晚些时候开庭审理。根据近年来支付佣金的计算，该案件的损害赔偿总额可能超过 400 亿美元。该行业的管理人员正在向 NAR 施压，要求其解决问题。

In November, NAR’s [chief executive stepped aside](https://www.wsj.com/real-estate/national-association-of-realtors-ceo-bob-goldberg-stepping-down-20aed3bc) a year ahead of his planned retirement. Wright, who NAR said is a turnaround expert but has little previous experience in real estate, stepped in as interim CEO. 
11 月，NAR 的首席执行官提前一年退休。NAR 表示，赖特是一位扭亏为盈的专家，但在房地产方面几乎没有经验，他担任了临时首席执行官。

Two months after that, [Kasper resigned](https://www.wsj.com/real-estate/realtor-associations-turmoil-continues-as-new-president-quits-a12dc566) after less than five months as president, alleging—with little explanation—that she was being blackmailed.
两个月后，卡斯珀在担任总裁不到五个月后辞职，她声称——没有太多解释——她遭到勒索。

Real-estate agents have begun dropping their NAR memberships, and the association is bracing for a financial pinch. Membership in January declined for the third straight month. In internal budget documents, the association projects an 8% decline this year, which it attributes to the [slow housing market](https://www.wsj.com/economy/housing/home-sales-likely-fell-to-15-year-low-in-2023-3da220e1). 
房地产经纪人已经开始放弃他们的 NAR 会员资格，而该协会正在为财务困境做准备。1 月份的会员人数连续第三个月下降。在内部预算文件中，该协会预计今年将下降 8%，这归因于房地产市场的低迷。

In many markets, real-estate agents can’t leave NAR without losing access to databases of real-estate listings. 
在许多市场中，房地产经纪人无法离开 NAR，否则将失去访问房地产清单数据库的权限。

Real-estate brokerage 

[Redfin](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/RDFN)

announced in October that it had resigned from the NAR board and was requiring its agents to leave the association wherever possible. So far, agents in only a couple of markets have been able to leave.
房地产经纪公司 Redfin 在 10 月份宣布已辞去 NAR 董事会职务，并要求其代理人尽可能退出该协会。到目前为止，只有几个市场的代理人能够退出。



More than 70% of real-estate agents surveyed in mid-October by 1000watt and BAM, a real-estate media company, said they would leave NAR if they could still view the listings databases and be able to give tours of homes for sale.
房地产媒体公司 1000watt 和 BAM 在 10 月中旬对房地产经纪人进行的调查显示，超过 70% 的受访者表示，如果他们仍然可以查看房源数据库并能够带人参观待售房屋，他们将退出 NAR。

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-925690?width=1260&height=840)

Dave Danforth, co-owner of Seattle-area brokerage Coldwell Banker Danforth, said some of his agents wanted to leave the National Association of Realtors. Photo: M. Scott Brauer for the Wall Street Journal
西雅图地区经纪公司 Coldwell Banker Danforth 的共同所有者戴夫·丹福斯表示，他的一些经纪人想要退出全国房地产经纪人协会。照片：M. Scott Brauer，华尔街日报

At Coldwell Banker Danforth, a brokerage in the Seattle area, some agents last year reached out to co-owner Dave Danforth saying they wanted to leave NAR. Danforth surveyed the brokerage’s more than 300 agents, and of those who responded, about two-thirds said their membership in the national and local Realtor associations provided little to no value. 
在西雅图地区的经纪公司 Coldwell Banker Danforth，去年一些经纪人联系了共同所有者 Dave Danforth，表示他们想离开 NAR。Danforth 调查了经纪公司的 300 多名经纪人，在做出回应的人中，约三分之二的人表示他们在全国和地方房地产经纪人协会的会员资格几乎没有价值。

Danforth, who had been a Realtor since 1990 and took over the brokerage from his mother, was frustrated that NAR policies made it difficult for some of his agents to leave and not others. 
自 1990 年以来一直担任房地产经纪人的 Danforth 从母亲那里接管了经纪公司，他感到沮丧的是，NAR 的政策让他的部分经纪人很难离开，而另一些经纪人却可以。

Coldwell Banker Danforth decided it wouldn’t renew its NAR membership in 2024. A few agents left over the decision, but others would have left if the brokerage had stayed in NAR, Danforth said. “I truly believe NAR needs to earn every agent’s membership fees, one by one, and if they did, it would be a much better organization,” he said.
Coldwell Banker Danforth 决定在 2024 年不续签其 NAR 会员资格。Danforth 说，一些经纪人因这一决定而离开，但如果经纪公司留在 NAR，另一些经纪人也会离开。“我真心认为 NAR 需要逐个赢得每个经纪人的会员费，如果他们做到了，它将成为一个更好的组织，”他说。

NAR has stepped up its fight this year. In late January, it released a video in which interim CEO Wright said, “NAR does not set commissions. It never has and it never will. Period. End of story.” 
NAR 今年加大了斗争力度。1 月下旬，它发布了一段视频，临时首席执行官赖特在视频中说，“NAR 不设定佣金。它从未设定过，也永远不会设定。句号。故事结束。”

The video was taken down shortly after it was posted. When it was put back up that evening, the phrase “it never has” had been edited out. 
视频在发布后不久就被删除。当晚重新发布时，“从未”一词已被编辑掉。

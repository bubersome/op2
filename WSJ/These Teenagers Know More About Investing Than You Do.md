# These Teenagers Know More About Investing Than You Do

## Custodial investment accounts for minors have surged in popularity 未成年人的托管投资账户的受欢迎程度大幅增长

![Rachael Kim and Kaida Benes](https://images.wsj.net/im-926799/?width=1278&size=1)

Sophia, a high school junior in the Chicago suburbs, invests in stocks such as Tesla, 

[Apple](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/AAPL)

and Amazon.com. When she started making money as a social-media content creator three years ago, her parents encouraged her to put some of her earnings in investments likely to grow over time, rather than parking all her cash in a [savings account](https://www.wsj.com/finance/investing/capital-one-high-interest-savings-complaints-b9adc1e9?mod=article_inline).
芝加哥郊区的高中三年级学生Sophia投资于特斯拉、苹果和亚马逊等股票。三年前，当她开始作为社交媒体内容创作者赚钱时，她的父母鼓励她将一部分收入投资于可能随时间增长的投资项目，而不是把所有现金存放在储蓄账户中。



She now has several thousand dollars invested in accounts set up by her father at Charles Schwab, Edward Jones and 

[Robinhood](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/HOOD)

. Last year, she saved up money to buy a new Tesla Model 3, which starts at around $40,000, through a payment plan she is splitting with her parents. On TikTok, Instagram and YouTube, she makes videos teaching her thousands of followers about investing basics.
她现在在她父亲在查尔斯施瓦布、爱德华琼斯和罗宾汉设立的账户中投资了几千美元。去年，她攒了钱买了一辆起价约为4万美元的特斯拉Model 3，通过与父母分期付款的方式。在TikTok、Instagram和YouTube上，她制作视频教她成千上万的追随者投资基础知识。



“I’ve always had a business mindset of wanting to make money, and I’m very OK with taking risk,” Sophia said. “There’s really no minimum age to start.”
“我一直都有着赚钱的商业思维，而且我非常愿意承担风险，”Sophia说道。“其实开始创业并没有最低年龄限制。”

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-926810?width=700&height=1050)

Sophia Castiblanco has invested money she earned from creating social-media content.
索菲亚·卡斯蒂布兰科已经投资了她通过创建社交媒体内容赚取的钱。

Sophia is one of many teenagers jumping into the U.S. stock market. Teens generally can’t open their own brokerage accounts until they turn 18, but adults can set up custodial accounts for minors. The accounts are turned over to the children when they reach legal age.
索菲亚是众多青少年之一，涌入美国股市。青少年通常在18岁之前无法开设自己的经纪账户，但成年人可以为未成年人设立托管账户。这些账户在孩子们达到法定年龄时移交给他们。

Custodial accounts for teens at Schwab totaled nearly 200,000 in 2022, up from about 120,000 in 2019, according to the company. They jumped above 300,000 in 2023, thanks in part to Schwab’s integration of TD Ameritrade. Other brokerages, including Vanguard, Fidelity and Morgan Stanley’s E*Trade, also reported a surge in custodial accounts in recent years.
根据该公司的数据，2022年施瓦布银行的青少年托管账户总数达到近20万，较2019年的约12万有所增加。由于施瓦布银行与TD Ameritrade的整合，2023年这一数字跃升至30万以上。其他券商，包括先锋、富达和摩根士丹利的E*Trade，近年来也报告了托管账户的激增。

Some teens ask their parents to open accounts—and share the login information—at brokerages such as Robinhood that don’t offer custodial accounts. At smaller financial apps such as Greenlight, teenagers are investing more money than ever before. They invested $20 million in 2023 using the Greenlight app, up from around $10 million in 2021. 
一些青少年要求父母在一些不提供托管账户的券商（如Robinhood）开设账户，并共享登录信息。在像Greenlight这样的较小的金融应用程序上，青少年投资的金额比以往任何时候都多。他们在2023年使用Greenlight应用程序投资了2000万美元，而2021年大约只有1000万美元。

A Fidelity study on teens and money recently estimated that about a quarter of teenagers in the U.S. have started investing, based on an online survey of 2,081 respondents ages 13 to 17. Trades placed using Fidelity’s Youth app, an account opened by parents but owned by teens, jumped in the fourth quarter. 
根据富达公司最近对美国青少年和金钱的研究估计，大约四分之一的美国青少年已经开始投资。该研究基于对2081名年龄在13至17岁之间的受访者进行的在线调查。使用富达公司的青少年应用程序进行的交易在第四季度大幅增加。

Many teenagers opened up their accounts during winter break while off from school, said Kelly Lannan, a senior vice president at Fidelity.
许多青少年在寒假期间开设了账户，这是Fidelity的高级副总裁Kelly Lannan说的。

The boom in teen trading is part of a wider rush to financial markets among Americans since the start of the Covid-19 pandemic. [Stocks rocketed higher](https://www.wsj.com/articles/why-did-stock-markets-rebound-from-covid-in-record-time-here-are-five-reasons-11600182704), drawing hordes of newbie investors trying to profit from the big gains. 
青少年交易的繁荣是自新冠疫情爆发以来美国人涌向金融市场的一部分。股票价格飙升，吸引了大批新手投资者试图从巨大的收益中获利。

Many of those new investors have since ditched the [meme stocks](https://www.wsj.com/articles/amc-other-meme-stocks-turn-options-market-upside-down-11623144602) that soared during that era but have remained invested, sending the share of [Americans who own stocks](https://www.wsj.com/finance/stocks/stocks-americans-own-most-ever-9f6fd963?mod=article_inline) to an all-time high. 
许多新投资者在那个时期疯狂上涨的模因股票后已经抛弃了它们，但仍然保持投资，使美国股市持有股票的比例达到历史最高水平。

Stocks are back at record levels, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average recently topping 38000 and the [S&P 500 eclipsing 5000](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/stock-market-today-dow-jones-earnings-02-08-2024/card/s-p-500-hits-intraday-record-of-5000-Icl9UiXV05h3I1EkiC59?mod=article_inline) for the first time. Since the start of 2020, when an unprecedented trading boom among rookie investors kicked off, the S&P 500 has soared around 55%. 
股票市场再次创下纪录水平，道琼斯工业平均指数最近突破了38000点，标普500指数首次超过5000点。自2020年初新手投资者引发了前所未有的交易繁荣以来，标普500指数已飙升约55%。

“There’s just more and more awareness that the sooner you start, the better things are,” said James Martielli, head of investment and trading services at Vanguard. 
“越早开始，事情就会越好，这一认识越来越普遍。”Vanguard的投资和交易服务主管詹姆斯·马蒂埃利说道。

Martielli said he opened custodial accounts for his three children more than a decade ago when they were toddlers. At Vanguard, there has been a jump in custodial IRAs, a type of retirement account. 
Martielli说他在十多年前为他的三个孩子开设了托管账户，当时他们还是蹒跚学步的孩子。在Vanguard，托管IRA（一种退休账户）的数量有所增加。

The biggest advantage is time. Setting aside $10 a week for a child at birth would leave an 18-year-old with a roughly $20,000 nest egg, assuming an 8% annual return, according to the investing app Stash, which offers custodial accounts. Left until the investor turned 70, and assuming annual growth of 8%, that sum would mushroom to about $1 million.
最大的优势是时间。根据提供托管账户的投资应用Stash的数据，从婴儿出生开始每周存入10美元，到18岁时将会有大约2万美元的积蓄，假设年回报率为8%。如果一直保留到投资者70岁，并假设年增长率为8%，那么这笔金额将会增长到约100万美元。

Of course, these returns can be shaped by many factors, including when an investor buys in and how stocks end up doing. The S&P 500 has recorded a 10% annualized total return over the past three decades, according to Dow Jones Market Data through the end of 2023. Buying an S&P 500 index fund at the peak of the dot-com bubble in 2000 would generate an annualized total return of around 7%, while buying at the low of the financial crisis in 2009 would lead to a roughly 16% annualized total return.
当然，这些回报可以受到许多因素的影响，包括投资者的购买时间和股票的表现。根据道琼斯市场数据，过去三十年来，标普500指数的年化总回报率为10%，截至2023年底。在2000年互联网泡沫达到顶峰时购买标普500指数基金将产生约7%的年化总回报率，而在2009年金融危机低点购买则将导致约16%的年化总回报率。

### Hot tech stocks 热门科技股

Brokerage executives say that technology behemoths that are ubiquitous in the lives of teens are often some of the most widely held shares. At Vanguard, U.S. stock index funds are particularly popular in custodial accounts.
经纪公司高管表示，在青少年生活中无处不在的科技巨头往往是持有股份最广泛的公司之一。在先锋集团，美国股票指数基金在托管账户中特别受欢迎。

Mahanth Komuravelli, 16, has a small chunk of his roughly $7,000 portfolio in an S&P 500 index fund, while most of his positions are in big companies such as Amazon and Advanced Micro Devices. He is exploring buying some small-cap stocks such as education company 

[Chegg](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/CHGG)

. Mahanth uses a Fidelity Youth Account that his father helped him open. The two often discuss investment ideas.
16岁的Mahanth Komuravelli大约有7000美元的投资组合，其中一小部分投资在标普500指数基金上，而大部分仓位则投资在亚马逊和高级微设备等大公司。他正在考虑购买一些小市值股票，比如教育公司Chegg。Mahanth使用的是他父亲帮助他开设的富达青少年账户。他们经常讨论投资理念。



“Sometimes he asks me for advice,” said Mahanth, a high school junior in Edison, N.J.
有时他向我征求建议，"Mahath在新泽西州爱迪生市的一所高中三年级学生说道。

Kaida Benes, a 13-year-old from the suburbs of Minneapolis, has been stashing money—earned from household chores such as doing the dishes or cleaning the bathroom—in an investment account on Greenlight that now has about $1,000 in it. 
来自明尼阿波利斯郊区的13岁的凯达·贝内斯一直在将通过做家务，如洗碗或清洁浴室所赚的钱存入Greenlight的投资账户中，目前账户里大约有1000美元。

She’s also been drawn to bigger companies and has invested in tech stocks such as Apple, 

[Alphabet](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/GOOG)

and streaming companies

and

. At times, she has been on edge about potential losses. She says her mother has helped her stomach the volatility. 
她也被大公司吸引，并投资于苹果、谷歌和流媒体公司迪士尼和Netflix等科技股。有时候，她对潜在的损失感到紧张。她说她的母亲帮助她承受了这种波动性。



“Stocks go up and down. It’s fine, it just happens,” Kaida said she’s learned. 
股票涨跌是正常的，这只是发生的事情，凯达说她已经学会了。

She has been hunting for other opportunities to make money to pour into savings or investments. She recently found a recliner chair at a yard sale and enlisted her parents to help fix it up and flip it for a profit on

marketplace, she and her mother, Renee Benes, said. 
她一直在寻找其他赚钱的机会，以便将钱存入储蓄或投资。她最近在一个庭院销售中找到了一把躺椅，并请她的父母帮助修理并在Facebook市场上翻转以获利，她和她的母亲Renee Benes说。



“I like having money,” Kaida said.
我喜欢有钱。

Renee Benes said she was frustrated that she didn’t learn about investing until a year or two ago, when she was well into her 30s. Benes, who’s an online influencer, wanted her daughter and son to be more financially savvy. 
Renee Benes说她感到沮丧的是，直到一两年前，她已经30多岁了，才开始了解投资。作为一个网络影响者，Benes希望她的女儿和儿子能更加懂得理财。

### Lessons learned 教训总结

Many young investors are starting to invest earlier than previous generations did. Almost two-thirds of Gen Z investors said they first started learning about investing in high school or middle school, compared with about 38% of millennials in a 2023 Bank of America survey of affluent individuals. Some are introduced to stocks through family members or teachers, while others have turned to social media.
许多年轻投资者开始比前几代人更早地进行投资。根据2023年美国银行对富裕人群的调查，近三分之二的Z世代投资者表示他们最早是在高中或初中开始学习投资，而这一比例在千禧一代中约为38%。一些人是通过家人或老师介绍股票投资的，而另一些人则转向社交媒体。

Felix Peng, a 17-year-old in the Los Angeles area, said he has learned a lot about investing from YouTube and Instagram—but that some social-media stars promote riskier trading strategies that seem more like gambling. He said it is a red flag when influencers try to sell expensive trading courses that promise investors they will make a lot of money quickly.
洛杉矶地区的17岁少年费利克斯·彭表示，他通过YouTube和Instagram学到了很多关于投资的知识，但一些社交媒体明星推广的高风险交易策略更像是赌博。他说，当社交媒体影响者试图销售昂贵的交易课程，承诺投资者能够快速赚很多钱时，这是一个警示信号。

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-926818?width=700&height=1050)

Felix Peng says he has learned that it is hard to time the market.
彭菲利克斯表示他已经意识到市场的时机很难把握。

Still, Felix believes it is beneficial for young people to learn from their mistakes when they have less money to lose. His investments in Apple, Meta Platforms and Alphabet have performed well. But when he bought shares of 

[Teladoc](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/TDOC)

around their peak and watched them tumble, he saw how tough it is to time the market. He has about $1,000 in a custodial account on Stockpile, an investing app geared toward parents and children.
尽管如此，费利克斯认为年轻人在没有太多金钱损失的情况下从错误中学习是有益的。他在苹果、Meta平台和Alphabet的投资表现不错。但当他在Teladoc股票达到峰值时购买了股票，然后看着它们下跌，他意识到市场的时机是多么艰难。他在Stockpile上的托管账户中大约有1000美元，这是一个面向父母和孩子的投资应用程序。



“It’s a great lesson and I’m glad I learned,” Felix said.
“这是一堂很棒的课，我很高兴我学到了，”费利克斯说。

Seventeen-year-old Rachael Kim in Orange County, Calif., traded shares of 

[AMC Entertainment Holdings](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/AMC)

during the meme-stock era and said she made a roughly 300% profit.
十七岁的Rachael Kim在加利福尼亚州的橙县交易了AMC Entertainment Holdings的股票，在迷因股票时代获得了大约300%的利润。



“For a little while, I got addicted to that adrenaline,” Rachael said of day trading. “But as I began researching more, I realized it was highly unlikely to continue that aggressive profit.”
“有一段时间，我对日内交易上瘾了，”Rachael谈到。 “但是随着我进行更多的研究，我意识到继续追求那种激进的利润是非常不可能的。”

Rachael said she started studying investing to help her parents, who are immigrants, prepare for retirement. Now she regularly invests about half of the money she makes—from creating social-media content, working as a cashier and teaching at her church—in index funds tracking the S&P 500 and tech-heavy Nasdaq-100. She has about $10,000 in her custodial Roth IRA at Fidelity.
Rachael说她开始学习投资是为了帮助她的移民父母为退休做准备。现在，她定期将自己赚的一半钱投资于追踪标普500指数和科技股密集的纳斯达克100指数的指数基金中。她在富达公司的托管罗斯IRA账户中有大约1万美元。

“Since we’re young, we have the privilege of seeing our investment compound,” Rachael said. “The biggest lesson would be to start early.”
“自从我们年轻以来，我们有幸看到我们的投资复利增长，”Rachael说。“最重要的教训就是要早早开始。”

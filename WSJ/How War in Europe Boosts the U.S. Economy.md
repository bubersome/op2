# How War in Europe Boosts the U.S. Economy

## European rearmament and American aid to Ukraine flow back to defense industrial base

![img](https://images.wsj.net/im-926467?width=700&height=466)

In the two years [since Russia invaded](https://www.wsj.com/world/europe/why-russias-war-in-ukraine-could-run-for-years-383f0bde?mod=article_inline) Ukraine, the U.S. defense industry has experienced a boom in orders for weapons and munitions. Business is coming from European allies trying to build out their military capabilities as well as from the Pentagon, which is both buying new equipment from defense manufacturers and replenishing military stocks depleted by deliveries to Ukraine.
自俄罗斯入侵乌克兰以来的两年里，美国国防工业经历了武器和弹药订单的激增。业务来自试图建立其军事能力的欧洲盟友以及五角大楼，五角大楼既从国防制造商那里购买新设备，又补充了因向乌克兰运送而耗尽的军事库存。

Industrial production in the [U.S. defense and space sector](https://www.wsj.com/politics/national-security/why-defense-contractors-are-saying-no-to-their-biggest-customer-the-pentagon-ad557306?mod=article_inline) has increased 17.5% since Russia launched its full-scale invasion of Ukraine two years ago, according to Federal Reserve data. 
根据美联储的数据，自俄罗斯两年前发动对乌克兰的全方位入侵以来，美国国防和航天领域的工业生产增长了 17.5%。

Biden administration officials say that of the $60.7 billion earmarked for Ukraine in a $95 billion supplemental defense bill, 64% will actually flow back to the U.S. defense industrial base. 
拜登政府官员表示，在 950 亿美元的补充国防法案中拨给乌克兰的 607 亿美元中，64% 将实际回流到美国的国防工业基地。

“That’s one of the things that is misunderstood…how important that funding is for employment and production around the country,” Lael Brainard, director of the White House National Economic Council, said in an interview Wednesday.
白宫国家经济委员会主任莱尔·布雷纳德在周三的采访中表示：“这是被误解的事情之一……该资金对全国就业和生产有多么重要。”

While war often has economic spinoffs, these are occurring without the U.S. actually doing any of the fighting.
虽然战争通常会产生经济影响，但这些影响是在美国实际上没有进行任何战斗的情况下发生的。

Recent spending by European governments on U.S. jet fighters and other military hardware represents “a generational-type investment. The past few years are equal to the prior 20 years,” said Myles Walton, a military industry analyst at Wolfe Research.
Wolfe Research 的军事产业分析师迈尔斯·沃尔顿表示，欧洲政府最近在美制喷气式战斗机和其他军用装备上的支出代表了“一代人的投资。过去几年相当于之前的 10 年。”

While the $95 billion aid package, which also includes funds for Israel and Taiwan, [passed the Senate](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/senate-on-verge-of-passing-95-3-billion-ukraine-israel-aid-package-55d07d5a) on Feb. 13, its fate is uncertain in the House, where it is opposed by Republican allies of former President [Donald Trump](https://www.wsj.com/topics/person/donald-trump), front-runner for the GOP presidential nomination.
虽然该 950 亿美元的援助计划（其中还包括对以色列和台湾的资金）于 2 月 13 日通过参议院，但其在众议院的命运尚不确定，在那里它遭到共和党盟友、前总统唐纳德·特朗普的反对，他是共和党总统候选人的领跑者。

Among their objections: The U.S. can’t afford to support Kyiv as federal deficits mount, Russia would prevail anyway, and the U.S. needs to better secure its own border before providing more aid overseas.
他们反对的理由：随着联邦赤字的增加，美国无力支持基辅，俄罗斯无论如何都会获胜，而且美国需要在向海外提供更多援助之前更好地保护自己的边境。

The latest money, on top of previous commitments, could inject funds worth about 0.5% of one year’s gross domestic product into the U.S. industrial defense base over several years.
除了之前的承诺外，最新的资金可以在几年内向美国的工业国防基地注入相当于一年国内生产总值约 0.5% 的资金。

The State Department recently said the U.S. made more than $80 billion in major arms deals in the year through September of which about $50 billion went to European allies—more than five times the historical norm, said Walton.
沃尔顿说，国务院最近表示，美国在截至 9 月份的一年中达成了超过 800 亿美元的重大军火交易，其中约 500 亿美元流向了欧洲盟友——是历史常态的五倍以上。

Poland has placed orders worth about $30 billion for Apache helicopters, High Mobility Artillery Rocket Systems, or Himars, M1A1 Abrams tanks and other hardware, the department said. Germany spent $8.5 billion on Chinook helicopters and related equipment, while the Czech Republic bought $5.6 billion of F-35 jets and munitions.
该部门表示，波兰已订购价值约 300 亿美元的阿帕奇直升机、高机动性火炮火箭系统（即海马斯）、M1A1 艾布拉姆斯坦克和其他硬件。德国斥资 85 亿美元购买奇努克直升机和相关设备，而捷克共和国购买了价值 56 亿美元的 F-35 战斗机和弹药。

The boost to the U.S. defense industry is just one way the fragmentation of the world economy along geopolitical lines [is tightening U.S.-European relations](https://www.wsj.com/articles/u-s-europe-trade-booms-as-old-allies-draw-closer-11668914679?mod=article_inline), often to the benefit of the U.S.
美国国防工业的提振只是世界经济沿着地缘政治路线分裂的一种方式，这种分裂正在加强美欧关系，通常对美国有利。

The cutoff of Russian gas supplies sent energy prices and inflation up sharply in Europe, while boosting European demand for U.S. liquefied natural gas. 
俄罗斯天然气供应中断导致欧洲能源价格和通胀大幅上涨，同时提振了欧洲对美国液化天然气的需求。

The U.S. became the world’s largest LNG exporter last year, and its LNG exports are expected to almost double by 2030 on already-approved projects. Around two-thirds of those [exports go to Europe](https://www.wsj.com/articles/american-fracked-gas-ukraine-invasion-europe-import-9e5cc725?mod=article_inline).
美国去年成为全球最大的液化天然气出口国，预计到 2030 年，其液化天然气出口量将在已获批准的项目中几乎翻一番。其中约三分之二的出口流向欧洲。

Five new LNG projects are being constructed in the U.S., representing investments of around $100 billion in total, said Alex Munton, director of global gas and LNG research at Rapidan Energy Group. Most of those projects only began construction after the start of the Ukraine war, he said, as the disruption to Europe’s gas supplies proved the value of LNG to potential backers and helped to move planned projects forward. “The U.S. economy benefits significantly because of these massive investments,” Munton said.
Rapidan Energy Group 全球天然气和液化天然气研究主管 Alex Munton 表示，美国正在建设五个新的液化天然气项目，总投资约为 1000 亿美元。他说，这些项目中的大多数都是在乌克兰战争开始后才开始建设的，因为对欧洲天然气供应的中断证明了液化天然气对潜在支持者的价值，并帮助推动了计划中的项目。“由于这些巨额投资，美国经济受益匪浅，”Munton 说。

Foreign direct investment to the U.S. increased by almost 50% between the 12 months through June 2021 and the same period in 2023, according to the Paris-based Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development, an association of market-based democracies. European companies in particular are lured by access to cheap and abundant energy.
根据总部位于巴黎的经济合作与发展组织（一个市场民主国家协会）的数据，截至 2021 年 6 月的 12 个月内，流入美国的外国直接投资增加了近 50%，而 2023 年同期的投资额也保持在同一水平。特别是欧洲公司被廉价且丰富的能源所吸引。

Military aid is, by itself, no economic panacea. While defense companies are adding jobs, those tied to Ukraine are a relatively small share of national employment and income. “I don’t think you can say confidently that the U.S. economy is larger as a result of the war, but certain sectors of the economy are certainly larger,” said Marc Goldwein, senior policy director for the Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget.
军事援助本身并不是经济灵丹妙药。虽然国防公司正在增加就业岗位，但与乌克兰相关的就业岗位在国家就业和收入中所占份额相对较小。负责联邦预算委员会的高级政策主管马克·戈德温说：“我认为你不能自信地说，由于战争，美国经济规模更大，但经济的某些部门肯定更大。”

The money also takes time to flow. Congress has to authorize funds to replenish Pentagon stocks, and then the Pentagon has to sign contracts for new equipment. Foreign sales of major weapons can take years and sometimes fall through. Poland’s annual military budget is about $16 billion, so it isn’t clear how the country can pay for $30 billion of new weapons orders anytime soon, said William Hartung, senior fellow at the Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft. 
资金流动也需要时间。国会必须授权拨款以补充五角大楼的库存，然后五角大楼必须签署新设备合同。主要武器的对外销售可能需要数年时间，有时还会失败。昆西负责任治国研究所高级研究员威廉·哈通表示，波兰的年度军费预算约为 160 亿美元，因此目前尚不清楚该国如何能够很快为 300 亿美元的新武器订单买单。

“We know that there are tens of billions in potential contracts on the table for U.S. firms based on the direct and indirect effects of the war in Ukraine, but it’s less clear how soon the companies will see those funds,” he said.

While European governments are paying for their orders, much of the spending is being financed by U.S. taxpayers or by borrowing, adding to the federal deficit. 
虽然欧洲政府正在为他们的订单付款，但大部分支出是由美国纳税人或借贷来资助的，从而增加了联邦赤字。

“Military spending has been crowding out other spending,” said Jason Furman, an economist at Harvard University. He noted that Vietnam War spending in the 1960s contributed to an overheating of the U.S. economy and high inflation.
“军费开支已经挤占了其他开支，”哈佛大学经济学家杰森·弗尔曼说。他指出，20世纪60年代的越南战争开支导致美国经济过热和高通胀。

The Biden administration, though, sees benefits. Military experts are concerned that decades of deindustrialization and miliary downsizing have left the defense industrial base unable to deliver the weapons and munitions necessary for a more dangerous world. Many [weapons systems orders are backlogged](https://www.wsj.com/articles/u-s-weapons-industry-unprepared-for-a-china-conflict-report-says-11674479916?mod=article_inline) for years.
不过，该管理层却获益匪浅。专家们担心，去工业化和军事裁军导致国防工业基地无法提供一个更加危险的世界所需的武器和弹药。许多武器系统订单积压多年。

The Ukraine war served as a warning for American defense strategists, said Cynthia Cook, a defense industry expert at the Center for Strategic and International Studies, a Washington-based think tank. “What Russia’s war pointed out relatively quickly is the constraints in the U.S. defense industrial base especially in terms of serving production rapidly. The good news is that this lesson has been learned when the U.S. is not directly at war.”
美国战略与国际研究中心国防产业专家辛西娅·库克表示，乌克兰战争对美国国防战略家来说是一个警示。“俄罗斯的战争很快指出了美国国防工业基础的制约，尤其是在快速提供生产方面。好消息是，当美国没有直接参战时，这一教训已经吸取到了。”

British defense contractor 

[BAE Systems](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/BAESY)

plans to create 500 jobs by expanding facilities in Minnesota, while 

[General Dynamics](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/GD)

will create around 120 position at a new plant in Texas, according to Cook.
英国防务承包商 BAE Systems 计划在明尼苏达州扩建设施，创造 500 个工作岗位，而通用动力公司将在德克萨斯州新建一家工厂，创造约 120 个职位，库克说。



Biden administration officials say funding allocated for Ukraine is rebuilding America’s defense industrial base, jump-starting and expanding production lines for weapons and ammunition, and supporting jobs in 40 states. 
拜登政府官员表示，分配给乌克兰的资金正在重建美国的国防工业基地，启动并扩大武器和弹药的生产线，并在 40 个州支持就业。

The administration might also hope for political benefits by noting the impact on employers in electoral swing states such as Pennsylvania and Arizona, each of which [will receive more than $2 billion](https://www.acq.osd.mil/news/spotlight/Ukraine-Infographic_15JAN2024.pdf), according to the Department of Defense.
政府也可能希望通过注意到对宾夕法尼亚州和亚利桑那州等摇摆州的雇主的影响而获得政治利益，根据国防部的数据，每个州都将收到超过 20 亿美元。
